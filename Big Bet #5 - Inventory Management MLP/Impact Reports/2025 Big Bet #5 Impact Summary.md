## Modal Screens: The Platform Capability That Unlocks Big Bets

### Why We Built This

Modal screens weren't built in isolation—they were built to **unlock strategic workflows across multiple teams and big bet initiatives**. Before modals, Method users faced a fundamental platform limitation: every interaction that required viewing or editing related records forced users out of their current context, disrupting workflow and creating friction.

This limitation wasn't just an annoyance—it was a **strategic blocker** for teams like customer management and service delivery.

The decision to build modals represented a **platform investment** that would pay dividends across the entire product organization. Rather than building one-off solutions for each team's specific use case, we built the foundational capability that enables all teams to deliver better user experiences.

### Quantitative Impact

**Adoption Metrics (2025):**
- **109 "Open Screen as Pop Up" actions** configured across 50 unique screens
- **50 unique customer accounts** actively using modals
- **13 admin users** adopted modals beyond PS team (including 3 Method partners)
- **Target exceeded:** Achieved 109 actions in 2 months vs. 3-month goal of 50 actions
- **Achievement:** 218% of target, delivered 33% faster than planned

**Features Delivered:**
- Open Screen as a Pop Up action in Action Editor
- Clear Screen functionality for modal workflows
- Refresh Grid controls for dynamic data updates
- Show Messages in Modals for contextual feedback
- Selectable Modal Sizes (Small, Medium, Large)

## Multi-Location Management: Building Inventory Excellence

### The Problem We're Solving

Multi-Location Management is our Big Bet #5 strategic initiative addressing a core customer pain point: **poor inventory visibility in Method CRM.**

**The core pain point:** Customers lack real-time visibility into their inventory. They can't see stock levels across multiple locations, track inventory movements, or confidently promise availability during sales—all without leaving Method or building expensive customizations. The result: manual workarounds, workflow friction, and operational inefficiency that limits their ability to grow.

**The business impact:** This pain point is contributing to significant churn in inventory-heavy verticals. In 2024, we lost 525+ accounts across key verticals:
- **Manufacturing, Wholesale & Distribution (MWD):** 199 accounts
- **Construction:** 175 accounts
- **Field Services:** 81 accounts
- **Retail:** 70 accounts

By delivering native multi-location management with real-time inventory visibility, we directly address this pain point and position Method competitively in verticals representing significant market opportunity.

### Phase 1: Internal Release & Foundation Building

**Timeline:** Shipped internally end of November 2025

**Features Delivered:**
- Multiple location/warehouse management
- Stock tracking per location
- Stock adjustments with audit trail
- Foundation for fulfillment workflows

**Strategic Decision:** Phase 1 was released internally to M11/M18 accounts to validate the technical foundation and gather stakeholder feedback.

### Demo Sessions: Excitement and Validation from Partners and PS

In November 2025, we conducted comprehensive demo sessions with Method partners and the PS team. The feedback was overwhelmingly positive—stakeholders validated we're building exactly what customers need.

### Phase 2: Bringing Inventory to Life

**Status:** Actively being built | Expected GA: March 2026

Phase 2 brings the full vision to life by embedding business rules and validations into all core screens/transactions.

**Features in Development:**

### The Impact This Will Have

Multi-Location Management directly addresses poor inventory visibility—a key driver contributing to the 525+ accounts we lost in 2024 across MWD, Construction, Field Services, and Retail verticals.

**Expected Impact:**
- **Reduces churn** by providing the real-time inventory visibility and multi-warehouse capabilities these customers require. With an average lifetime value (LTV) of $2,390.54 per cancelled account, even modest churn reduction translates into significant revenue protection. For example, a 50% reduction in churn within the MWD vertical alone would preserve roughly 100 accounts, equating to about $238,000 in LTV retained.
- **Enables market expansion** into the ~25,000 potential MWD customers (we currently have 850 accounts, 3.4% market share)
- **Positions Method competitively** against CRM alternatives in verticals where inventory management is table-stakes
- **Creates foundation** for future Priority 2 and Priority 3 features (reorder points, backorder management, detailed location tracking)
- **Validates the Hybrid Approach** as the right strategy for inventory (core features native, integrations for advanced needs)

### Key Achievement

The November 2025 demos with partners and PS validated our product vision and confirmed we're building what customers need. With Phase 1 shipped internally and Phase 2 actively being built, we're on track to deliver a complete inventory management solution in March 2026 that directly addresses the poor inventory visibility contributing to churn in our key verticals.
