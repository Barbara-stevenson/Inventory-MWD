## 5. Managing Expectations

| Constraint | Impact / Detail |
|------------|-----------------|
| **This is a "Native Foundation" launch** | We are launching with the core capabilities needed for 1-3 warehouse operations with moderate SKU complexity (1,000-5,000 SKUs). This version supports **non-regulated, non-perishable, non-serialized finished goods** only. Advanced features like serial number tracking, lot tracking, and assemblies are not included in GA. |
| **Stock Transfers NOT in GA** | Stock Transfers (moving inventory between locations) will be delivered in a future MLP batch. While users can track inventory by location in GA, they cannot yet initiate structured transfers. Workaround: Users can manually adjust stock at both locations and document the reason. |
| **Reorder Points, Low Stock Alerts, Backorder Awareness = MLP Phase 2/3** | GA focuses on visibility and control. Proactive alerting (low stock notifications, reorder point triggers) and backorder management (handling unfulfilled commitments) will be added in subsequent phases. |
| **Advanced Location Tracking (Bin Management) NOT in GA** | GA supports warehouse-level tracking (e.g., "Main Warehouse," "Satellite Storage"). Sub-location tracking (e.g., Aisle 3, Bin 12) and detailed bin management will be added in MLP Phase 3. |
| **QuickBooks Advanced Inventory Limitations** | Due to QuickBooks SDK constraints, Method cannot access QB Advanced Inventory features (Site/Bin/Location fields). Method becomes the source of truth for inventory; QB remains the source of truth for accounting only. This is by design to maintain inventory integrity. |

## 6. Use Case / Customer Story

**Scenario:** User X wants to achieve Y. Previously they had to do Z. Now, with [New Feature], they simply click [Button] and achieve Y instantly.

| Scenario | (Before) | (After) |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **A Sales Operations Coordinator creating a Sales Order for a multi-warehouse customer.** | The user creates a Sales Order in Method without knowing what inventory exists, where it's located, or what's actually available to sell. To check stock levels, they must leave Method to: (1) Log into QuickBooks, (2) Navigate to the Item List, (3) Find each item, (4) Check QoH, (5) Call the warehouse to confirm what's already committed to other orders and what's actually available. If stock is split across two warehouses, they must coordinate with warehouse staff to determine which location will fulfill the order. The sales team cannot confidently sell or close deals. The entire process takes 10-15 minutes and introduces errors. | The user opens the **Sales Order** screen in Method and sees real-time inventory visibility directly in the core transaction: **QoH, Committed, Expected, and Available stock** for each item. They select **"Warehouse A"** from the location dropdown. The system shows: "Widget A: QoH 100, Committed 50, Expected 75, **Available 125**." The user now confidently knows what inventory exists, where it's located, and what's actually available to sell—all within the core transaction. They add 80 units to the order with confidence—no phone calls, no QuickBooks lookups, no leaving Method. The sales team can confidently sell and close the deal. The order is created in 2 minutes. |
