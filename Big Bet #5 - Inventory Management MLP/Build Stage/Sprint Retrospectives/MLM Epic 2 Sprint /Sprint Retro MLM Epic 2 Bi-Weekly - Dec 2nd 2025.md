# Sprint Retro - December 2nd, 2025

![Retrospective Board](./Retro%20Visual%20Nov%207%202026.png)

## Sprint Overview

This retrospective was conducted for the Sales Management team during the MLM (Multi-Location Management) Epic 2 bi-weekly sprint. The team gathered to reflect on progress, celebrate successes, and identify actionable improvements for the ongoing sprint work. This was the second bi-weekly retrospective, building on feedback from the first retro that emphasized the importance of more frequent check-ins throughout the sprint.

## What Went Well 🎯

- **Process Improvements Taking Effect:** The communication channels and processes established from the first retrospective feedback are showing positive impact. The team is proactively surfacing problems earlier in the sprint rather than discovering them late.

- **Effective Blocker Communication:** Team members are consistently communicating blockers and progress updates with the right people at the right time, preventing issues from being drawn out or delaying work unnecessarily.

- **Documentation and Support:** The team is actively documenting changes as they occur and providing support where needed, ensuring knowledge is shared and decisions are tracked.

- **Continuous Improvement Mindset:** The team embraced the bi-weekly retrospective cadence, demonstrating commitment to getting "1% better" with each iteration.

## Areas for Improvement 🔧

- **Solution Validation with Technical Leadership:** Need to run complete solution workflows by a Technical Lead (TL) or appropriate Subject Matter Expert (SME) once designs are consolidated. This would minimize surprises and confirm that proposed workflows will function as designed.
  - **Specific Need:** Consult domain experts early in the process
  - **Impact:** Would prevent discovering technical limitations or incorrect assumptions late in implementation

- **Collaborative Solution Design:** Rather than immediately proceeding with the first solution idea, discuss potential approaches with other developers who have worked in similar code areas to select the optimal solution.
  - **Trade-off Acknowledged:** This adds some developer time overhead upfront, but prevents wasting significantly more time later due to suboptimal decisions

## Action Items 📋

1. **Solution Review Process:** When working on complex workflow changes, run consolidated designs by appropriate SMEs before implementation:
   - Goal: Confirm assumptions and validate that workflows will function as designed

2. **Peer Review for Solutions:** Discuss solution approaches with other developers who have worked on similar code areas before proceeding with implementation (applies to complex changes, not simple fixes)

3. **Continue Communication Practices:** Maintain the effective practices established for surfacing blockers and progress updates early and with the right stakeholders

## Key Takeaways

This sprint demonstrated the team's commitment to continuous improvement and proactive communication. The bi-weekly retrospective cadence is successfully surfacing issues earlier, allowing the team to address them before they become significant blockers. The primary challenges center around technical validation in the absence of a dedicated Technical Lead and managing the flow of P2 tickets alongside sprint work.

Moving forward, the focus will be on establishing a more structured solution review process with domain experts, clarifying release strategies for mixed sprint/kanban work, and resolving the multi-location management technical blockers. The team's strong communication foundation positions them well to implement these process improvements while maintaining delivery momentum.
