# Inventory Management Accounting Loop

## Problem Statement

Customers cannot close the loop between inventory operations and accounting transactions in Method. Without a structured connection between Sales Order/Purchase Order fulfillment and Invoices/Bills, businesses must manually reconcile fulfillment workflows with financial records — creating operational inefficiency and breaking the order-to-cash cycle.

---

## Problem Space

### Background & Rationale

Now that Method supports multi-location inventory with Sales Orders and Purchase Orders driving committed and expected stock, customers need the accounting layer — Invoices and Bills — to participate in the same workflow. Today, Invoices and Bills exist independently from Method's inventory tracking, meaning there is no structured connection between fulfilling an order and recording its financial counterpart.

Without an accounting loop:

- There is no mechanism to convert delivered Sales Order items into Invoices or received Purchase Order items into Bills
- Deleting or undoing fulfilled orders has no guardrails against corrupting linked accounting records
- Sales Order and Purchase Order statuses do not reflect invoicing or billing progress, forcing users to open individual records to understand financial state
- Transaction conversions (Estimates to Invoices, Sales Orders to Purchase Orders) lack integration with the fulfillment workflow

A proper accounting loop ensures that inventory fulfillment and financial recording work as a unified workflow — enforcing data integrity between orders and accounting documents, and giving users real-time visibility into both operational and financial status. The accounting loop is a core, table-stake capability for any inventory system that bridges fulfillment and accounting.

### How do we know this is a real problem & worth solving?

The accounting loop is the natural next step after multi-location inventory and stock transfers. Every MWD business that fulfills orders must eventually invoice customers and record vendor bills. Without a structured connection between fulfillment and accounting transactions, the inventory data captured during fulfillment becomes disconnected the moment a financial document is created — undermining the accuracy that multi-location management was built to deliver.

This is further validated by the existing Sales Order and Purchase Order fulfillment workflows already in Method. Users who deliver items from specific locations expect that when they invoice those delivered items, the fulfillment context carries forward. The same applies to receiving inventory against Purchase Orders and recording vendor Bills.

A real example illustrates this clearly:

#### Brown Safe

**Pain Point:**
Brown Safe manages high-value inventory across multiple locations and requires precise tracking of which warehouse items ship from when fulfilling orders. Without a structured connection between fulfillment and accounting, their team must manually cross-reference fulfillment records with accounting documents, creating delays in billing and risk of discrepancies between what was shipped and what was financially recorded.

**Solution:**
With the accounting loop, Brown Safe can convert delivered Sales Order items directly into Invoices, maintain accurate inventory balances without manual reconciliation, and track invoicing progress directly from the Sales Order — eliminating the gap between fulfillment and accounting.

Closing the accounting loop is critical for inventory accuracy, workflow completeness, and long-term retention among MWD customers who rely on Method for their full order-to-cash cycle.

### Who are we building for?

We are building for small (1-100 employee) B2B distribution and wholesale companies that use Method's multi-location inventory to fulfill Sales Orders and Purchase Orders and need their accounting transactions (Invoices and Bills) to be connected to the fulfillment workflow across 1-3 warehouses.

### Goals

1. **Close the Order-to-Cash Loop**: Ensure Invoices and Bills are connected to the fulfillment workflow, linking delivered/received items to accounting documents without manual reconciliation.
2. **Maintain Inventory and Accounting Integrity**: Prevent users from deleting or undoing fulfilled items that are already linked to accounting documents, protecting both inventory accuracy and financial records.
3. **Provide Real-Time Financial Visibility**: Surface invoicing and billing status directly on Sales Orders and Purchase Orders so users can track financial progress without opening individual records.
4. **Preserve Data Across Conversions**: Ensure transaction conversions (SO to Invoice, PO to Bill, Estimate to Invoice, SO to PO, Invoice to PO) carry forward item and quantity data so records remain accurate.

---

## Solution Space

Extend Method's inventory management to include accounting transactions connected to the fulfillment workflow and enforce data integrity between fulfillment and financial workflows:

- Enforce that **Invoices and Bills do not impact Method's inventory balances** — inventory is tracked through SO/PO fulfillment, while Invoices and Bills handle accounting via QBO
- Enable **Sales Order to Invoice** and **Purchase Order to Bill** conversion flows that carry forward delivered/received items
- Add **invoice and bill status tracking** on Sales Orders and Purchase Orders with automatic status updates
- Enforce **delete and undo guardrails** that prevent corruption of linked accounting records
- Support **transaction conversions** (Estimate/Opportunity to Invoice, SO to PO, Invoice to PO)
- Support **transaction duplication** that preserves item data

**Important:** Invoices and Bills sync to QuickBooks Online for accounting purposes. Method's inventory balances (QoH, Committed, Expected) are managed exclusively through Sales Order and Purchase Order fulfillment workflows — saving an Invoice or Bill does not alter Method inventory stock. Invoices and Bills do not have location dropdowns — location-level inventory tracking is handled exclusively through Sales Orders and Purchase Orders.

## Non-Functional Requirements

The following non-functional requirements outline performance, reliability & integrity, security, responsiveness, accessibility, data validation, and usability standards for this experience.

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Performance** | • Invoice and Bill save operations must complete in < 500ms for transactions with <= 50 line items.<br>• Create Invoice and Create Bill modal must load in < 500ms with up to 50 delivered/received line items.<br>• SO/PO list view columns (Invoice Status, Bill Status) must render in < 300ms on page load. |
| **Reliability & Integrity** | • System must maintain 99.99% accuracy in inventory quantity tracking (Committed, Expected, QoH, Available) across all locations when processing accounting loop transactions.<br>• Invoice and Bill save operations must have zero impact on Method inventory balances (QoH, Committed, Expected) — inventory changes occur exclusively through SO/PO fulfillment.<br>• Delete and undo operations on SOs/POs must be blocked when linked accounting documents exist, with zero exceptions.<br>• Invoice and bill status transitions (Not Invoiced/Billed, Partially, Fully) must update atomically with the corresponding accounting action.<br>• QBO sync events (Invoice/Bill deletion) must propagate status changes to linked SOs/POs with no orphaned states. |
| **Security** | • All Invoice and Bill conversion and duplication actions must respect existing transaction-level permissions. |
| **Responsiveness** | • Invoice and Bill screens must function on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.<br>• Create Invoice/Bill modals must be usable on screens >= 768px wide. |
| **Accessibility** | • All new form fields must have clear labels and support keyboard navigation (Tab, Enter, Escape).<br>• Color contrast for validation messages and error states must comply with WCAG 2.1 AA standards (minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text).<br>• Modal dialogs (Create Invoice, Create Bill, Undo) must trap focus and support Escape to close. |
| **Data Validation** | • Transaction conversions must enforce required fields before save is permitted. |
| **Usability** | • Validation errors must clear immediately when the user corrects the invalid field.<br>• Non-inventory and service items must not block transaction saves. |

## Out of Scope

The following items will not be addressed in this sprint to keep the experience focused and deliverable:

- Location selection on Invoices and Bills (location-level inventory tracking is handled exclusively through Sales Orders and Purchase Orders)
- Partial quantity invoicing (invoicing less than full delivered quantity per line item)
- Partial quantity billing (billing less than full received quantity per line item)
- Invoice/Bill editing after save
- Credit notes or refund workflows
- Returns management
- Inventory valuation or COGS calculations
- Multi-currency support for conversions
- Batch/bulk invoicing or billing from list views
