# Stock Transfers

## Problem Statement

Customers cannot reliably move inventory between warehouses in Method. Without stock transfers, businesses must manually adjust quantities at each location — a process that is error-prone, slow, and nearly impossible to audit.

---

## Problem Space

### Background & Rationale

Now that Method supports multiple warehouses, customers need a reliable, structured way to move stock between locations. In real-world distribution operations, inventory is constantly shifting — from main warehouses to satellite sites, to trucks, and back again.

Without a native transfer flow:
- Users must manually decrease stock in one warehouse and increase it in another via stock adjustments
- There is no traceability or audit history for what moved, when, or by whom.
- There is no validation to prevent negative stock or accidental over-transfers.
- Sales, purchasing, and fulfillment workflows quickly fall out of sync with actual on-hand inventory.

A proper stock transfer function standardizes these movements, maintains accuracy across all warehouses, and provides the operational visibility required for downstream workflows. Stock transfers are a core, table-stake capability of any inventory system that supports multi-location management.

### How do we know this is a real problem & worth solving?

While we don't have feature requests or cancellation reasons specifically tied to "stock transfers," the capability is a core, table-stake feature in every modern inventory platform and fundamental to how MWD companies operate. Any business managing more than one warehouse must move stock between locations to run efficiently — and they expect their inventory system to support that natively.

A real example illustrates this clearly:

#### Euroboor

**Pain Point:**
Euroboor struggled with inadequate inventory management features in both QBO and Method. They needed a solution that could handle advanced inventory functions. Without these capabilities, they faced challenges in managing inventory allocations and accurately tracking inventory movements, leading to inefficiencies and potential errors.

**Solution:**
The solution involved building out a full suite of custom inventory management features inside Method. A key part of that solution included stock transfer capabilities, enabling Euroboor to reliably move inventory between locations while maintaining traceability and accurate stock levels.

Stock transfers are a critical pillar for inventory accuracy, workflow reliability, and long-term retention for our target vertical.

### Who are we building for?

We are building for small (1–100 employee) B2B distribution companies that sell non-regulated, non-perishable, and non-serialized finished goods with moderate SKU complexity (1000–5000 SKUs total) across 1–3 warehouses.

### Goals

1. **Maintain Accurate Multi-Location Inventory**: Ensure inventory moves between sites cleanly with zero manual adjustments.
2. **Enable Operational Efficiency**: Streamline daily workflows that rely on moving stock and reduce errors.
3. **Reduce Churn & Improve Competitive Positioning**: Increase Method's value proposition as a viable all-in-one solution for SMBs in our core ICP.
4. **Foundation for Advanced Workflows**: Provide a scalable core inventory infrastructure that can integrate with Katana for businesses that outgrow in-house capabilities.

---

## Solution Space

Deliver a native stock transfer solution directly within Method:

1. Move quantity from one location to another
2. In Transit and Received Statuses
3. Update inventory quantities for source and destination locations

**Important:** Stock transfers are a Method-only feature and do not sync to QuickBooks Online. They exist solely within Method to manage multi-warehouse inventory movements.

---

## Success Metrics

| Category | Metric | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Adoption (Habit Formation)** | 20% of Wholesale & Distribution accounts completing ≥3 stock transfers (Received status) within 30 days of first transfer creation | Measures true adoption and repeated value among the ICP by focusing on completed transfers—the point at which real inventory movement and operational impact occur. |
| **Workflow Health** | 40% of transfers created as 'Open' that are marked as Received within 60 days of creation | Measures whether planned workflows complete successfully — ensuring that transfers initiated as ‘Open’ progress to the final, value-delivering Received state. |

## Non-Functional Requirements

The following non-functional requirements outline performance, reliability & integrity, security, responsiveness, and accessibility for this new experience.

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Performance** | • Stock transfer creation and save operations must complete in < 500ms for transfers with ≤ 50 line items.<br>• Stock transfer status changes (Open → In Transit → Received) with inventory updates must complete in < 1 second for transfers with ≤ 50 line items.<br>• Available quantity calculations at source and destination locations must refresh in < 300ms after stock-impacting event on save.<br>• Support up to 100 active locations without performance degradation in location dropdowns or quantity calculations.<br>• System must handle 1,000+ concurrent stock transfers without performance degradation or race conditions in inventory quantity updates. |
| **Reliability & Integrity** | • System must maintain 99.99% accuracy in quantity tracking (Committed, Expected, Quantity on Hand, Available) across all locations at all times with zero tolerance for phantom inventory or mismatched quantities.<br>• Stock transfer deletions and reverts must atomically reverse all inventory impacts with no residual Committed or Expected quantities remaining.<br>• Multiple line items for the same item must independently track and update inventory quantities without duplicate adjustments.<br>• Changes to location status (active/inactive) must not alter historical stock transfer data; transfers with inactive locations must display validation warnings per FR-801.<br>• Changes to item status (active/inactive) must not alter historical stock transfer data; transfers with inactive items must display validation warnings per FR-802.<br>• Stock Transfer # uniqueness must be enforced at the database level to prevent duplicate IDs across concurrent users. |
| **Security** | • All stock transfer actions (create, edit, delete, revert) must respect the Items app "Manage" permissions model as defined in FR-900. |
| **Responsiveness** | • Stock transfer screens (New/Edit, List view) must function on desktop, tablet and mobile devices. |
| **Accessibility** | • All form fields must have clear labels and support keyboard navigation (Tab, Enter, Escape).<br>• Color contrast for validation messages and error states must comply with WCAG 2.1 AA standards (minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text).<br>• Focus states for all interactive elements must be clearly visible and meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards. |
| **Data Validation** | • Real-time validation must prevent saving transfers with invalid data (duplicate Stock Transfer #, same source/destination, zero/negative quantities, insufficient available quantity). |
| **Usability** | • Validation errors must clear immediately when the user corrects the invalid field. |
