# Top 200 MWD Customer Workflow Analysis

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines the core workflow patterns across the top 5 industry categories by company count and LTV. Understanding these workflow types helps identify the operational needs and inventory management requirements for each vertical.

### Top 5 Industries Analyzed
1. **Industrial Equipment & Machinery** - 37 companies (19.3% of total LTV)
2. **Construction & Building Materials** - 25 companies (17.6% of total LTV)
3. **Medical & Healthcare** - 17 companies (10.7% of total LTV)
4. **Electronics & Technology** - 14 companies (8.5% of total LTV)
5. **Consumer Goods & Retail** - 13 companies (6.9% of total LTV)

**Total Coverage:** 106 companies representing 53% of the top 200 accounts

---

## Workflow Pattern Categories

Workflows have been grouped into 6 core patterns:

| Workflow Pattern | Description |
|------------------|-------------|
| **Manufacturing (Specialized/Custom)** | Custom fabrication, specialized manufacturing, handcrafted products |

| **Distribution + Service** | Distribution combined with installation, maintenance, and support |

**2. Hybrid Models Common**
- **25%** of companies combine multiple workflows (Manufacturing + Distribution, Distribution + Service)
- Businesses are vertically integrated to control quality and customer experience
- Requires inventory systems that handle both production and distribution

**3. Service Integration**
- **21.7%** include service/rental components (Distribution + Service, Sales Service & Rental)
- Equipment-based businesses require maintenance, parts, and support
- Need for serialized inventory tracking and service history

**4. Distribution Channels**
- **33.4%** have wholesale/distribution components
- Multi-location warehousing is common
- Real-time inventory visibility across locations is critical

### Workflow Pattern Definitions

**Manufacturing (Specialized/Custom):** Companies that primarily manufacture custom or specialized products. Includes FDA-regulated manufacturing, handcrafted products, and made-to-order production.

**Manufacturing + Distribution:** Hybrid businesses that both manufacture products and distribute third-party brands. Requires both production and wholesale capabilities.

**Wholesale/Distribution:** Companies focused on distributing products from multiple manufacturers. Includes parts distributors, multi-brand dealers, and import/export businesses.

**Distribution + Service:** Distributors who also provide installation, maintenance, and technical support services. Common in equipment and medical device industries.

**Sales, Service & Rental:** Equipment-focused businesses offering sales, parts, service, and rental operations. Requires serialized inventory and availability tracking.

**Custom Design & Fabrication:** Design-centric businesses that create custom products based on customer specifications. Heavy in construction (kitchens, stone) and specialized industries.
