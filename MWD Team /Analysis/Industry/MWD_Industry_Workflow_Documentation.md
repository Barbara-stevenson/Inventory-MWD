# MWD Industry Workflow Documentation

**Document Date:** 2026-01-12
**Source Data:** MWD_Top_200_Enriched.csv + Daily App Page Loads (366 days)
**Purpose:** Document how each industry vertical uses Method CRM in their daily operations

---

## Document Overview

This documentation maps the **actual observed workflows** of MWD customers based on:
- Industry classification from enrichment research
- Daily app usage patterns (366 days of data)
- Logical business process flows (not just frequency)

### Key Difference from Previous Analysis

**Previous:** Apps ranked by total clicks (Activities always first because it's logged constantly)
**Now:** Apps arranged in **logical business process order** with actual usage data

Example: A manufacturer's workflow is:
1. **Contacts** → Add/update customer
2. **Activities** → Log inquiry
3. **Estimates** → Create quote
4. **Sales Orders** → Convert to order
5. **Work Orders** → Track production
6. **Invoices** → Bill customer

Even if Activities has more total clicks, Contacts is the logical starting point.

### Workflow Sequencing Logic

Apps are ordered based on typical business process flow:

**Lead to Cash Flow:**
1. Contacts → Customer information
2. Activities → Communication logging
3. Opportunities → Pipeline tracking
4. Estimates → Quoting
5. Sales Orders → Order processing
6. Work Orders → Production/service
7. Items → Inventory management
8. Purchase Orders → Procurement
9. Invoices → Billing
10. Payments → Cash collection

**Variations by Workflow Type:**
- **Manufacturing:** Emphasizes Work Orders and Items
- **Distribution:** Emphasizes Items and Purchase Orders
- **Service:** Emphasizes Work Orders and Field Crew
- **Retail:** Emphasizes direct customer interaction

### Key Findings

1. **Workflow gaps are widespread:** Many accounts missing critical apps for their workflow type
2. **Items app adoption crisis:** <30% across manufacturing industries despite inventory needs
3. **Purchase Orders underused:** Distribution workflows lack procurement automation
4. **Work Orders rare:** Manufacturing workflows tracking jobs manually
