# MWD Customer Profile & Behavior: Comprehensive Synthesis Report

**Report Date:** January 13, 2026
**Data Coverage:** 200 top MWD accounts | 366 days app usage | $6.6M total LTV
**Purpose:** Synthesize WHO customers are, WHAT they do with Method CRM, and HOW they operate

---

## Executive Summary

This report synthesizes 5 comprehensive data sources to create a complete picture of Method CRM's Manufacturing, Wholesale & Distribution (MWD) customer base. All findings are observation-based from actual customer data—no assumptions or inferences about unmet needs.

### Data Scope & Methodology

**Full Dataset (200 companies):**
- Industry categorization across 23 categories
- LTV and company count analysis
- Product complexity distribution (77% High/Very High)
- App usage patterns (157 companies with complete 366-day data)

**Deep Dive Subset (106 companies from Top 5 industries):**
- Detailed product category taxonomy
- Workflow pattern analysis
- Complexity driver mapping
- Represents 53% of total customer base, 63% of total LTV

**Why the subset?** The most granular analysis (product categories, complexity drivers, workflow archetypes) was performed on the top 5 industries that represent the majority of the MWD business. Statistics marked with **(Top 5)** come from this 106-company deep dive.

---

### Key Findings at a Glance

**WHO THEY ARE:**
- 200 companies across 23 industry categories, $6.6M total LTV
- **77% operate at High or Very High product complexity** (200 companies)
- Top 3 industries: Industrial Equipment (19%), Construction (18%), Medical (11%)
- Average 10 users per account, $33K average LTV

**WHAT THEY USE:**
- 64.5% actively use Contacts app (most adopted) (157 with complete data)
- 56% use Invoices, 45.5% Estimates, 39.5% Sales Orders
- **Only 22% use Items app despite inventory-heavy operations**
- **Only 15% use Purchase Orders, 6.5% Work Orders**
- High-health accounts use platform 18X more than critical health accounts

**HOW THEY OPERATE (Top 5 Industries - 106 companies):**
- **43.4% are custom/specialized manufacturers**
- **33.4% have distribution in their workflow** (wholesale/distribution business model)
- 21.7% provide service/rental operations
- Dominant workflow: Contact → Quote → Order → Invoice (core CRM)
- Inventory/operations apps severely underutilized

## The Universal MWD Workflow

### Lead-to-Cash Process (Used by 80%+ of Customers)

```
1. CONTACTS (64.5% adoption)
   └─ Add/update customer and vendor information

2. ACTIVITIES (52.2% adoption)
   └─ Log interactions: calls, emails, meetings

3. OPPORTUNITIES (27.4% adoption) [Optional]
   └─ Track deal pipeline and sales stages

4. ESTIMATES (45.5% adoption)
   └─ Generate quotes and proposals

5. SALES ORDERS (39.5% adoption)
   └─ Convert quotes to orders

6. INVOICES (56.1% adoption)
   └─ Bill customers for products/services

7. PAYMENTS (20.4% adoption)
   └─ Record and apply customer payments
```

**Observation:** This is the **core CRM workflow** with strong adoption at each stage. Customers successfully use Method for relationship management and transactional processing.

- FDA-regulated: **29.4%** of medical companies

**Industry-Specific Patterns (Top 5 Industries):**

- **Electronics:** Most diversified workflows, lowest operational adoption overall

## The Critical Insight

**MWD customers successfully use Method CRM for relationship management and transactional processing (lead-to-cash workflow).**

**BUT the vast majority do NOT use operational apps (Items/PO/WO) despite:**
- 77% operating at High/Very High complexity (200 companies)
- 43.4% being custom manufacturers (Top 5 industries)
- 34% having supply chain complexity (Top 5 industries)
- 33.4% having distribution in their workflow (Top 5 industries)

**This represents either:**
1. A massive **untapped adoption opportunity** if barriers can be removed
2. A **product-market fit signal** that native operational apps don't serve complex workflows
3. A **market reality** that customers prefer external systems (ERP, MES) for inventory/production
