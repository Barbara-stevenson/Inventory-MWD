# Per-Persona Messaging
### Card 06 | Section 2: Positioning & Messaging

**Audience:** Sales reps (talk tracks, email personalization), marketing (content targeting, ad copy), BDRs (outreach sequencing)
**When to use:** You know which persona you're talking to and need the right language, proof, and angle for that specific buyer

**Agent:** Marcus — Message Architect | Messaging Squad
**Supporting:** Riya — ICP Mapper

## How to Use This Card

Each persona section gives you four things:

1. **Their core message** — the one sentence that matters most to this buyer
2. **Pillar priorities** — which of the four Yes pillars to lead with (ordered by impact)
3. **Proof points** — real customer evidence mapped to this persona's concerns
4. **Language guide** — what to say and what not to say

Every message here connects to the Message House (Card 05b). The roof stays the same. What changes is which pillar you lead with and how you frame the proof.

## Persona 1: Operations Manager / VP of Operations

**Their world:** They manage the flow. Every order, every handoff, every location. They're the system — and they're tired of being the system. They live in QuickBooks and spreadsheets, switching between tabs all day.

### Core Message

"Method connects your entire operation — quotes, orders, production, delivery — in one system that syncs with QuickBooks. You stop being the middleman."

### Pillar Priority Order

1. **Yes to Quote-to-Cash** — this is their daily pain. Every manual handoff is their problem.
2. **Yes to Real World Business** — their workflows are complex and specific. Template software fails them.
3. **Yes to QuickBooks** — they need to know QB stays and data flows automatically.
4. **Yes to Expert Support** — they want someone who understands operational complexity.

### Proof Points for Ops Managers

| What They Care About | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Connecting the full order flow | Duraline: estimates → sales orders → invoices flow with equipment tracking | Customer interview |
| Multi-location visibility | Mobility City: standardized 54 franchise locations, centralized ops view | Customer data |
| Eliminating manual handoffs | Brown Safe: entire production cycle was paper-based before Method | Customer interview |
| Custom workflow matching | Europena: store-level iPad audits, rolling data apps, two mirrored companies | Customer interview |
| Mobile field access | Vintage Makers: "Driving down the road and all the information was on Method" | Direct quote |
| Real-time dashboards | Top 200 MWD accounts average 50+ custom screens — operational visibility is the norm | Account analysis |

### Language Guide — Ops Manager

**Use these phrases:**
- "One system for your entire operation"
- "From quote to delivery — tracked, automated, visible"
- "Your team enters data once. Method and QB handle the rest."
- "Custom dashboards for the KPIs you actually track"
- "Build it around your workflow, not ours"

**Avoid these phrases:**
- "Streamline your operations" (buzzword)
- "All-in-one solution" (not accurate)
- "Replace your current systems" (triggers resistance)
- "Easy setup" (they know better — complex operations mean complex implementation)

### Discovery Questions

- "Walk me through what happens after a customer says yes to a quote."
- "How many people touch an order before it ships?"
- "How do you track what's happening across your locations right now?"
- "What's the one report you wish you had that you can't build today?"

## Persona 2: Owner / General Manager

**Their world:** They built this business. They know every customer, every bottleneck, every cost. Technology decisions are personal — bad software costs real money. They've been burned before and they take their time.

### Core Message

"Method gives you the visibility and control you need to grow — without replacing QuickBooks, hiring IT, or paying enterprise prices."

### Pillar Priority Order

1. **Yes to QuickBooks** — their QB investment is sacred. Lead here to build trust immediately.
2. **Yes to Expert Support** — they want a partner, not a vendor. DEP resonates.
3. **Yes to Real World Business** — they need to know it adapts to their specific operation.
4. **Yes to Quote-to-Cash** — important but they think in bigger pictures, not specific workflows.

### Proof Points for Owners

| What They Care About | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Keeping QuickBooks | Every single interviewed customer chose Method partly because of QB sync | All five interviews |
| Enterprise alternative | Vintage Makers rejected NetSuite: "too heavy and expensive for small business" | Direct quote |
| Affordable customization | Brown Safe: "Ability to customize was a priority" — chose Method over enterprise options | Customer interview |
| Predictable costs | DEP: $325/month for 3 hours of dedicated expert time. No scope surprises. | Pricing |
| Proof from similar businesses | Brown Safe (manufacturing), Europena ($25M food distribution), Mobility City (54 locations) | Customer profiles |
| ROI math | $600-$1,200/month for a typical team vs. enterprise ERP at $5K-$20K/month | Pricing comparison |

### Language Guide — Owner / GM

**Use these phrases:**
- "Keep QuickBooks for accounting. Add Method for everything else."
- "Enterprise-level customization at a fraction of the price"
- "We build it with you — $325 a month for a dedicated expert"
- "Businesses like yours use Method to [specific outcome]"
- "Your team's been building workarounds. Method makes them permanent."

**Avoid these phrases:**
- "Digital transformation" (corporate buzzword — they'll tune out)
- "Cutting-edge technology" (they want proven, not cutting-edge)
- "Quick ROI" (they've heard this before — show don't tell)
- "You need to upgrade from QuickBooks" (deal killer)

### Discovery Questions

- "What's the biggest bottleneck slowing your growth right now?"
- "How do you currently track the health of your business — what do you look at every morning?"
- "Have you looked at enterprise systems before? What happened?"
- "How many systems does your team use on a typical day?"

## Persona 3: Office Manager / Administrative Lead

**Their world:** They're the glue. They enter data into QuickBooks, chase invoices, update spreadsheets, and fix things when processes break. They know the actual workflows better than anyone because they execute them every day. They'll use Method the most — and resist it the hardest if it adds work.

### Core Message

"Method syncs with QuickBooks automatically — so you stop entering data twice and start trusting one system instead of five."

### Pillar Priority Order

1. **Yes to QuickBooks** — QB sync is make-or-break for this persona. Lead with it every time.
2. **Yes to Quote-to-Cash** — they feel the pain of manual handoffs most directly.
3. **Yes to Expert Support** — DEP means they won't be left alone to figure it out.
4. **Yes to Real World Business** — customization matters but they think "will this work?" before "can this flex?"

### Proof Points for Office Managers

| What They Care About | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Eliminating double entry | QB two-way sync: every interviewed customer cited this as a core reason for choosing Method | All five interviews |
| Reliable sync | Duraline: "We kind of use it all the same" — 15 users across sales, shipping, customer care, AP, AR | Customer interview |
| Not learning a nightmare system | DEP: a dedicated expert who helps set things up and trains them | Pricing / positioning |
| Replacing spreadsheet dependency | Europena: multiple custom apps replaced manual tracking across two companies | Customer interview |
| Getting hours back | Vintage Makers: mobile invoicing eliminated manual data re-entry from field | Customer interview |
| Being included in decisions | Duraline's cross-department adoption makes the admin role central, not peripheral | Customer data |

### Language Guide — Office Manager

**Use these phrases:**
- "You enter it once. QuickBooks and Method stay in sync automatically."
- "Everything your team needs, in one place — no more tabbing between five windows"
- "We'll set it up with you. A real person, not a help desk."
- "Your QB data doesn't change. Method adds the layer on top."
- "It's built to save you time, not add to your plate."

**Avoid these phrases:**
- "It's easy" (they've heard this before every failed implementation)
- "You'll love it" (they'll decide that for themselves)
- "Just trust us" (trust is earned through the demo, not words)
- "You won't need your spreadsheets anymore" (threatening — those spreadsheets are their safety net)

### Discovery Questions

- "What does your typical day look like — walk me through the data you touch?"
- "How much time do you spend on double entry between QB and other tools?"
- "What's the thing you track in a spreadsheet that you wish was automatic?"
- "What happened the last time you implemented new software?"

## Cross-Persona Messaging Matrix

Use this when you're not sure who you're talking to — or when multiple personas are in the room.

| Message Element | Ops Manager | Owner / GM | Office Manager |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lead with** | Workflow automation | Business visibility + cost | QB sync + less data entry |
| **Prove with** | Multi-location dashboards, quote-to-cash flow | Customer references, pricing comparison | Live QB sync demo, time savings |
| **Close with** | "Build your exact workflow" | "Enterprise power, SMB pricing" | "One system, no double entry" |
| **Fear to address** | "Will it handle our complexity?" | "Is this worth the investment?" | "Will this make my job harder?" |
| **DEP angle** | "They'll build your operational system" | "A partner who knows your business" | "They'll train you and build it with you" |
| **Campaign line use** | "Yes, there's a Method for [their specific workflow]" | "Yes, there's a Method for that — without the enterprise price tag" | "Yes, there's a Method for that — and it syncs with QuickBooks" |

## When Multiple Personas Are in the Room

This happens often. The Owner brings the Ops Manager to validate. The Office Manager shows up because she'll be the one using it.

**Rule:** Address the Owner's concerns first (they have veto power), validate with the Ops Manager (they have influence), and reassure the Office Manager (they have adoption power).

**Opening:** "Let me show you three things — the business-level view [Owner], the operational workflow [Ops], and what daily use looks like with QB sync [Office Manager]."

**Demo sequence:**
1. Executive dashboard → Owner sees visibility
2. Quote-to-work-order flow → Ops Manager sees automation
3. Live QB sync → Office Manager sees no double entry
4. Custom screen builder → Everyone sees flexibility
5. DEP overview → Owner sees partnership, Ops sees capability, Office Manager sees support
