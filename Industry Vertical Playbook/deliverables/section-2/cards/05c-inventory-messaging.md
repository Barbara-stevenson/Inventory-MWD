## Why This Card Exists

Inventory is the #1 topic MWD buyers raise — and the #1 place where overpromising costs trust.

Method is NOT a standalone inventory management system. It does not have native BOM, kitting, lot tracking, or shop floor control. Saying otherwise will lose the deal faster than being honest about it.

This card gives you the honest, effective way to talk about inventory. Use it every time.

---

## The Honest Position

**What Method does with inventory:**

Method surfaces QuickBooks' built-in inventory features inside your CRM and operational workflows. Your inventory data lives in QuickBooks. Method brings that data into the same place your sales team manages customers, quotes, and orders — so everyone can see what's available without calling the warehouse.

**What Method does NOT do:**

- No standalone inventory management system
- No native BOM (bill of materials)
- No kitting or assembly management
- No lot tracking or serial number management (native)
- No shop floor control
- No native e-commerce integration

**How to say it:** "Method extends your QuickBooks inventory into your operational workflows. For complex inventory needs beyond what QB offers, we have two proven paths."

## The Two Paths for Complex Inventory Needs

When MWD buyers need more than QB's built-in inventory, two approaches work — and both have real customer evidence behind them.

### Path 1: Third-Party Inventory Integration via API

Connect a dedicated inventory tool to Method through API integration. The inventory tool handles the heavy lifting — Method handles CRM, operations, and the customer-facing workflows.

**Real example:** Europena connected Fishbowl (now transitioning to Markov) for food distribution inventory management. Inventory data flows into Method and QuickBooks.

**Honest caveat:** Third-party integrations add complexity. Europena's Fishbowl experience showed that data structure mismatches created manual cleanup work — product descriptions came through as generic line items. The switch to Markov is aimed at cleaner data flow. Integration quality varies by tool.

**Best for:** Businesses with complex inventory needs (multi-warehouse, lot tracking, BOM) that need a purpose-built inventory system alongside Method.

### Path 2: Custom Builds Inside Method

Build custom inventory tracking directly in Method — warehouse locations, stock levels, serial numbers, bin locations. Real businesses have done this.

**Real examples:**
- Duraline built custom Equipment tracking with purchase history, commission tracking, and status management
- Vintage Makers tracks 3,400+ SKUs across 6 states with inventory quantities in QB Desktop Enterprise
- Mobility City built custom rental management and equipment tracking across 54 franchise locations

**Honest caveat:** Custom builds require DEP investment and time. They work for specific workflows but don't replace a full inventory management system. The more customization you add, the more ongoing maintenance matters.

**Best for:** Businesses that need specific inventory visibility (equipment tracking, custom status workflows, location-based stock views) but don't need full MRP capabilities.

## The Script: How to Handle the Inventory Conversation

### When They Ask "Do You Do Inventory?"

**Don't say:** "Yes, Method has inventory management."
**Don't say:** "No, we can't help with inventory."

**Say this:** "Method works with your QuickBooks inventory data — it brings stock levels, item details, and availability into the same place your team manages customers and orders. For businesses that need more than QB's built-in inventory, we have two proven approaches: connecting a dedicated inventory tool via API, or building custom tracking inside Method. What does your inventory workflow look like today?"

### When They Need Deep Inventory (BOM, Kitting, Lot Tracking)

**Say this:** "For BOM, kitting, and lot tracking, you'll want a dedicated inventory tool — Fishbowl, Katana, or similar. Method connects with those tools so your inventory system handles production while Method handles CRM, quoting, order management, and the customer-facing side. One of our food distribution customers runs this exact setup."

### When They Need Basic Inventory Visibility

**Say this:** "If your main need is seeing what's in stock when you're quoting or taking orders, Method pulls that directly from QuickBooks. Your sales team sees real-time availability without leaving the CRM. No double entry, no calling the warehouse."

### When They Compare You to Fishbowl, Katana, or Similar

**Say this:** "Those are excellent inventory tools — and they do things Method doesn't, like BOM and lot tracking. The difference is they're not CRM systems. Method handles your customer relationships, quoting, order management, and operational workflows. Many MWD businesses need both — a dedicated inventory tool for production and Method for everything customer-facing. They work together."

## What QB Version Matters

This is important and comes up often. QuickBooks versions have different inventory capabilities — and that directly affects what Method can surface.

| QB Version | Inventory Capabilities Method Can Surface |
|---|---|
| QBO Simple Start / Essentials | Basic inventory tracking (quantities, cost) |
| QBO Plus / Advanced | Inventory Parts, categories, reporting |
| QB Desktop Pro / Premier | Inventory Parts, groups, units of measure |
| QB Desktop Enterprise | Advanced Inventory (multi-location, serial/lot, FIFO), Assembly Items, barcode scanning |

**Key point for sales:** If a prospect is on QB Desktop Enterprise, they already have more inventory capability than most people realize. Method extends that into their operational workflows. If they're on QBO, the native inventory is simpler — and complex needs will require Path 1 or Path 2.

## Messaging Rules for Inventory

1. **Never claim native inventory management.** Method extends QB inventory. That's different from managing inventory natively.
2. **Always offer both paths.** Third-party API integration and custom builds inside Method. Never present just one.
3. **Lead with honesty, follow with proof.** Acknowledge the gap, then show how real customers solve it.
4. **Don't avoid the conversation.** MWD buyers expect to talk about inventory. Dodging it looks evasive.
5. **Match the response to the need.** Basic visibility? QB sync handles it. Complex MRP? They need a dedicated tool alongside Method. Custom tracking? DEP can build it.
6. **Know the QB version.** Desktop Enterprise users have way more inventory capability than QBO users. Ask early.
