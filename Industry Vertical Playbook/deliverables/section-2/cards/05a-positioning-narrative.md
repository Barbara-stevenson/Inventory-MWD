## The Core Positioning Statement

**For** manufacturing and wholesale/distribution businesses running on QuickBooks that have outgrown spreadsheets but aren't ready for enterprise ERP,

**Method is** the Customizable CRM that extends QuickBooks into a full operational system —

**So that** you get the workflows, visibility, and automation your business needs without replacing your accounting, hiring developers, or spending six figures on software that takes a year to set up.

## The Strategic Narrative

### The Problem (What MWD Buyers Live With)

Most MWD businesses run on QuickBooks and spreadsheets. It's not ideal, but it works — until it doesn't.

You add a second warehouse and suddenly nobody knows what's in stock where. You hire a third sales rep and quoting becomes a game of telephone. Your best admin builds a spreadsheet system so complex that nobody else can use it — and when she's on vacation, things break.

The pain isn't one dramatic moment. It's the slow accumulation of workarounds — the triple data entry, the Friday afternoon reporting sessions, the phone calls to the warehouse, the quotes with wrong margins. Each one costs a little time. Together they cost a lot of money.

### The Trap (Why Buyers Get Stuck)

When MWD businesses start looking for something better, they hit a wall.

On one side: **enterprise ERP.** NetSuite, Dynamics 365, SAP. Comprehensive, powerful — and designed for companies ten times your size. $5K–$20K per month. Six to twelve months to implement. You'd have to leave QuickBooks. You'd need a consultant to make changes. One MWD owner we spoke with called it "too heavy and expensive for small business."

On the other side: **general-purpose CRM.** Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho. Great for sales pipelines — but they don't understand work orders, production tracking, multi-location inventory, or quote-to-fulfillment workflows. And none of them offer real two-way QuickBooks integration.

In the middle: **specialized inventory tools.** Fishbowl, Katana, inFlow. Deep on inventory, shallow on everything else. No CRM. No customer management. No operational workflows.

The result? Most MWD businesses stay stuck. They know spreadsheets aren't the answer, but nothing they find fits the actual shape of their business.

### The Method Answer

Method sits in the gap between "too much" and "not enough."

We don't replace QuickBooks — we extend it. Method connects to your QB data with real two-way sync, then adds the operational layer QB is missing: custom workflows, work orders, mobile access, quotes, dashboards, and whatever else your business needs built.

The difference is customization. Not the "choose from three templates" kind. Real customization — custom screens, custom fields, custom automations built around how your business actually works. Our customers have built custom production tracking apps, equipment management systems, multi-company operational dashboards, and franchise compliance workflows. All inside Method, all syncing with QuickBooks.

And when the customization gets complex, our Dedicated Enhancement Plan (DEP) means we build it with you. $325/month for a dedicated expert who learns your business and builds your system — not a help desk ticket that disappears into a queue.

### Why This Matters for MWD Specifically

Manufacturing and distribution businesses are hybrid by nature. They make things and move things. They quote, produce, ship, install, and service. They operate across locations. Their workflows don't fit neatly into "sales CRM" or "accounting software" or "inventory tool."

Method works for MWD because it doesn't force you into a predefined box. You build the system that matches your actual operation — whether that's a 12-week production cycle for custom safes, food distribution across retail accounts with store-level audits, or medical equipment that gets sold, leased, rented, and serviced.

Five things make Method the right fit for MWD:

1. **QuickBooks stays.** Your accounting doesn't change. Method handles operations. Two-way sync keeps everything connected.

2. **Your workflows, not ours.** Custom screens, fields, and automations that match how your team actually works — not how a software company thinks you should work.

3. **Visibility across locations.** See orders, customer activity, and operational data across every warehouse and office in one place.

4. **Quote-to-cash in one flow.** Estimate to sales order to invoice to payment to work order — connected, automated, synced with QB.

5. **Expert support, not ticket queues.** DEP gives you a dedicated consultant who knows your business. Real people who treat you like a partner.

## Positioning Guardrails

These keep MWD messaging aligned with Method's master positioning. Check any piece of MWD copy against these.

### Always True
- Method is a Customizable CRM. Not ERP. Not inventory software. Not a project management tool.
- Method extends QuickBooks — never replaces it.
- Customization is the core differentiator. Not features. Not price. Customization.
- "Yes, There's a Method for That" is the campaign line. Use it.
- The four pillars apply to MWD: Yes to real world business, Yes to quote-to-cash, Yes to QuickBooks, Yes to expert support.

### Never Claim
- Method is NOT a standalone inventory management system. (See Card 05c for how to talk about inventory.)
- Method does NOT have native BOM, kitting, lot tracking, or shop floor control.
- Method does NOT have native e-commerce integration.
- Don't call Method an "ERP" or "ERP alternative" — it positions us against tools where we lose.
- Don't claim "all-in-one" — we're the operational layer on top of QB, not a replacement for everything.

### Competitive Framing
- **vs. Enterprise ERP:** "Enterprise-level customization at SMB pricing. Keep QuickBooks. Implement in weeks, not months."
- **vs. Traditional CRM:** "Built for operations, not just sales. Work orders, custom workflows, and QB sync included — not bolted on."
- **vs. Specialized tools:** "CRM + operations + QB integration in one system. No more stitching together five tools that don't talk to each other."
- **vs. Status quo (QB + spreadsheets):** "You've outgrown spreadsheets. Method is the next step — without the enterprise leap."
