## Story 1: Brown Safe — Custom Manufacturing

**Company:** Brown Safe Manufacturing
**Industry:** Manufacturing (custom safes and vaults)
**Size:** ~40-year-old company, QuickBooks Desktop user
**Method footprint:** 177 custom screens, custom Production app

### The Challenge

Brown Safe manufactures custom safes with a 12-week production cycle. Every build is different — custom dimensions, custom features, custom timelines. Before Method, the entire production tracking process was paper-based. The owner needed a system that could track long production cycles without replacing QuickBooks or paying enterprise prices.

They looked at enterprise solutions. Too expensive. Customization would have required outside developers at high hourly rates.

### What They Built in Method

- A custom **Production app** that tracks every build from order through the full 12-week manufacturing cycle
- **177 custom screens** across their operation
- Quote-to-production workflows connected to QuickBooks Desktop
- All inventory and accounting data staying in QBDT — Method handles the operational side

### Why It Works

Brown Safe's owner made customization the priority from day one. Method let them build a system that matches how they actually manufacture — not how software companies think manufacturing should work.

> "Ability to customize was a priority."

### Best Used For

- Prospects in **custom manufacturing** with long production cycles
- Buyers who've been quoted enterprise prices and walked away
- Conversations about customization depth — 177 screens proves this is real
- **Owner/GM persona** — the decision was driven by the owner's need for operational control at affordable pricing

## Story 2: Duraline Systems — Manufacturing + Distribution + Service

**Company:** Duraline Systems
**Industry:** Medical equipment manufacturing, distribution, and service
**Size:** 15 active users across every department
**Method footprint:** System of record, active DEP since Nov 2024, custom apps for Equipment, Commission, Purchase History, Email Status

### The Challenge

Duraline is a hybrid business — they manufacture, distribute, and service medical equipment. That means sales, shipping, customer care, AP, and AR all need to work from the same data. Before Method, information lived in different places depending on the department. Orders moved through too many handoffs.

### What They Built in Method

- **System of record** across the entire company — not just sales
- Custom apps for **Equipment tracking**, Commission calculations, Purchase History, and Email Status
- Estimates → sales orders → invoices flowing with equipment tracking at every step
- Active **DEP engagement** with monthly consulting — their dedicated expert (Alex) builds and adjusts the system as needs evolve

### Why It Works

Duraline doesn't use Method as a CRM. They use it as the operational hub for the entire business. Every department — sales, shipping, customer care, AP, AR — works in Method.

> "Alex is constantly customizing to our needs."
> "We kind of use it all the same."

### Known Friction

Be honest about these if asked:
- Email marketing suspended at ~3,000 contacts. Must batch sends at 800.
- Performance slows as customization grows: "The more we make it customizable, it becomes heavy and slower."
- Reporting: most reports still run in QB because Method reporting is less intuitive for some use cases.
- QB sync occasionally needs manual re-runs.

### Best Used For

- Prospects who are **hybrid businesses** (manufacture + distribute + service)
- Conversations about **cross-department adoption** — Duraline proves Method scales beyond sales
- **DEP value pitch** — real, ongoing partnership with tangible results
- **Ops Manager persona** — the operational hub framing resonates
- Objection handling when prospects worry Method is "just CRM"

## Story 3: Vintage Makers — Manufacturing + Distribution

**Company:** Vintage Makers
**Industry:** Custom wine cellar manufacturing + wholesale cigar distribution
**Size:** Founded 1994, multi-state operations (6 states), QuickBooks Desktop Enterprise
**Method footprint:** Custom workflows, mobile field access, 3,400+ SKUs managed through QBDT

### The Challenge

Vintage Makers runs two distinct operations under one roof: custom wine cellar manufacturing and wholesale cigar distribution across 6 states. Field sales reps visit accounts on the road and need access to customer history, pricing, and inventory. The owner tried NetSuite — rejected it as "too heavy and expensive for small business."

### What They Built in Method

- **Mobile field access** — sales reps pull up customer history, pricing, tax calculations, and generate invoices on the road using tablets
- Custom workflows for quoting and delivery across multiple states
- Inventory quantities tracked in QBDT Enterprise, accessible in Method for real-time quoting
- Works with their customization consultant (Yaser) to build apps, buttons, and simplified flows

### Why It Works

The owner is the champion. He uses Method daily, tells everyone about it, and relies on the customization to match how his business operates across two very different product lines.

> "I have yet to find anything that I can't get customized."
> "It's a game changer. I tell everybody it's awesome."
> "Driving down the road and all the information was on Method and when I showed the customer they were impressed."

### Best Used For

- Prospects who are **multi-state distributors** with field sales teams
- Conversations about **mobile access** — the "driving down the road" quote is powerful
- **Enterprise alternative** pitch — rejected NetSuite, chose Method
- **Owner/GM persona** — the owner IS the champion and the story reads like their world
- **QB Desktop Enterprise** users — validates that Method works with the most advanced QB version

## Story 4: Europena — Food Distribution

**Company:** Europena
**Industry:** Food distribution (transitioning into food manufacturing)
**Size:** $25M revenue, two company entities, QuickBooks Desktop
**Method footprint:** CRM, store-level audits, rolling data apps, Web to Lead, two mirrored companies

### The Challenge

Europena is a $25M food distribution company serving retail accounts. They operate two company entities and needed every workflow mirrored across both. Their operation involves complex handoffs — PO to sales order to invoice with inventory checks at multiple steps. They needed store-level auditing in the field using iPads and trade show lead capture.

### What They Built in Method

- **Two mirrored company entities** with matching apps and workflows across both
- **iPad audit workflows** — sales reps visit stores and complete audits with photos and notes on the spot
- **Rolling data apps** for tracking sales performance across retail accounts
- **Web to Lead** for trade show demand capture
- Custom reporting and contact management layered on top of QuickBooks Desktop

### Why It Works

Europena considered switching away from Method at one point. The accumulated customization investment kept them — the cost of rebuilding everything elsewhere was too high. QuickBooks was one of the main reasons for choosing Method in the first place.

> "The greatest thing is… we are able to customize."
> "We have actually the two companies… we can kind of mirror every app."

### Known Friction

Be honest about these if asked:
- Third-party inventory integration (Fishbowl) created data structure issues — product descriptions came through as generic "Fishbowl item" entries, requiring manual cleanup. Now transitioning to Markov for cleaner data flow.
- Too many workflow steps: "We have a lot of steps… it would be nice to cut them down."

### Best Used For

- Prospects in **food and beverage distribution** — highest LTV sub-industry ($953K)
- **Multi-company operations** — two entities, mirrored workflows
- **Field audit / iPad use cases** — store-level data collection in the field
- **Inventory conversation** — honest example of third-party integration, including the challenges
- **Retention / stickiness proof** — customization investment makes switching painful

## Story 5: Mobility City — Distribution + Franchise

**Company:** Mobility City
**Industry:** Medical equipment distribution + franchise operations
**Size:** 54 franchise locations, 3 regional offices (Boca Raton, Springfield, Milpitas)
**Method footprint:** 159 custom screens, dedicated consulting since Aug 2022, centralized compliance

### The Challenge

Mobility City operates 54 franchise locations selling, repairing, and renting medical equipment. Before Method, each franchise ran its own processes — no standardization, no central visibility, no compliance tracking. The corporate team needed to bring consistency across all locations without forcing franchisees off their existing systems.

### What They Built in Method

- **159 custom screens** standardizing operations across all 54 franchise locations
- Centralized **compliance tracking** — drove compliance from 0% to 66% (with ongoing work)
- Custom **rental management** and equipment tracking workflows
- Centralized dispatch and operations view across three regional offices
- 33 total Method users across the franchise network

### Why It Works

Craig at Mobility City drove the standardization effort from corporate. Method's customization let them build the exact operational framework the franchise needed — without forcing every location to change their QuickBooks setup.

### Known Friction

Be honest about these if asked:
- Compliance is at 66%, not 100%. Some franchisees revert to QB or paper processes.
- Franchise adoption is uneven — some locations are fully in Method, others resist.

### Best Used For

- Prospects with **franchise or multi-location operations** — 54 locations is compelling
- **Scale and complexity proof** — 159 custom screens is the most in any customer
- **Compliance and standardization** conversations
- **Ops Manager persona** — Craig's role and approach is a direct match
- **Long-term DEP partnership** — dedicated consulting since Aug 2022 shows ongoing value
