# Mia's PMM Review — MWD Marketing Toolkit
### Head of Product Marketing | March 13, 2026

**Scope:** Full review of the MWD Marketing Toolkit (Hub + Cards M1–M8)
**Review lens:** Does this toolkit serve the GTM motion? Is research informing messaging? Do the cards connect so a marketer can actually execute?

---

## Overall Verdict

**This toolkit is solid and usable.** The hub routes people well, the tone is consistently Method, inventory is handled honestly across every card, and the customer proof is well-organized. A marketer could open this today and start building campaigns.

That said, there are gaps between cards — places where the connection between research, messaging, and execution is implicit rather than explicit. A strong marketer will figure it out. A new hire won't. The fixes below are about closing those gaps.

---

## What's Working

**1. Inventory honesty is airtight.** Every card that touches inventory gets it right — M1 says "customization story," M6 has a clear do/don't claim section, M7 Job 5 has an honest disclaimer, and the hub's "Five Things to Always Get Right" nails it at #3. This was the biggest risk in the toolkit and it's handled well.

**2. Customer proof is well-organized and usable.** M3's quick-reference table ("which customer story for which angle") means a marketer doesn't have to read every snapshot to find what they need. The anonymized-with-brackets approach gives them the story externally and the real name internally. Good structure.

**3. Tone is consistent.** No buzzwords spotted. No puns. The psychographic notes in M2 reinforce why the tone matters ("these aren't SaaS buyers browsing G2"), and M1's "Lines to Avoid" section gives concrete guardrails. Vera would approve.

**4. Competitive angles are honest.** M4 gives credit where it's due — Odoo's MRP is acknowledged, HubSpot's marketing tools are respected, NetSuite is framed as right-sized rather than bad. The "when to choose [competitor]" recommendation for the SEO page structure is the kind of honesty that builds trust with MWD buyers.

**5. The hub works.** "Five Things to Always Get Right" is the most valuable section in the entire toolkit. If a marketer reads nothing else, that section prevents the worst mistakes.

## Issues to Fix

### Issue 1: M5 campaign line could imply native inventory
**Card:** M5 Content Playbook — "Yes, There's a Method for That" campaign variations
**The line:** "Need inventory visibility across 3 warehouses without leaving QuickBooks? Yes, there's a Method for that."
**The problem:** This reads like Method has a native inventory feature. It technically doesn't — the "method" is customization or API integration. A reader who sees this ad and then learns Method doesn't ship with inventory will feel misled.
**Fix:** Reframe to: "Need to track inventory your way — without leaving QuickBooks? Yes, there's a Method for that." This keeps the customization angle without implying a native module.

### Issue 2: M6 pricing range assumes CRM Pro minimum without saying so
**Card:** M6 Sales Quick-Reference — Pricing comparison stats
**The line:** "Typical MWD team (5-10 users): ~$225-$730/month on Method"
**The problem:** The low end ($225) is 5 users × $45 (CRM Pro), not 5 users × $27 (Contact Management). This is probably the right assumption for MWD teams who need quoting and invoicing, but the card doesn't explain why Contact Management is excluded from the range.
**Fix:** Add a parenthetical: "~$225-$730/month on Method (CRM Pro to Enterprise — most MWD teams need at least CRM Pro for quoting and invoicing)"

### Issue 3: Competitor pricing needs date stamps
**Cards:** M4 and M6
**The problem:** Pricing comparisons ("NetSuite year 1: $30,940-$80,940+", "Salesforce Manufacturing Cloud: $475/user/month") have no source or date. Competitive pricing changes. A marketer who uses these numbers in content 6 months from now might be citing outdated figures.
**Fix:** Add a note at the bottom of M4 and M6's pricing sections: "Competitive pricing based on published rates as of Q1 2026. Verify before using in time-sensitive content."

### Issue 4: M4 is missing Microsoft Dynamics and Monday.com
**Card:** M4 Competitive Angles
**The problem:** The method-info skill lists both as competitors (the "Overall" competitor set includes "Salesforce, NetSuite, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, Zoho, Monday"). M4 covers NetSuite, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, and Odoo — but skips Dynamics and Monday.
**Why it matters:** Dynamics is relevant for larger MWD companies evaluating ERP-adjacent CRM. Monday is increasingly showing up in the "project management as CRM" space. A marketer building comparison content will eventually need these angles.
**Fix:** Add Dynamics 365 and Monday.com sections to M4. Even short ones (4-5 lines each) would close the gap. Dynamics angle: "enterprise complexity + cost vs. Method's QB-first simplicity." Monday angle: "project management tool trying to be CRM vs. CRM built for operations."

## Strategic Gaps

These aren't errors — they're missing connections that would make the toolkit stronger.

### Gap 1: No persona → content stage mapping
**What exists:** M2 describes three personas and what content works for each. M5 lists content topics by intent level (high-intent, problem-aware, thought leadership).
**What's missing:** A matrix connecting them. Which M5 topics serve which M2 persona at which evaluation stage? The Owner reads different content than the Office Manager. A new marketer has to make these connections themselves.
**Suggested fix:** Add a "Persona × Funnel Stage" matrix to either M2 or M5 — a simple table mapping each persona to recommended content at awareness, evaluation, and decision stages.

### Gap 2: Buying triggers aren't connected to email sequences
**What exists:** M6 lists 5 buying triggers (new location, key person left, quoting errors, visibility gap, QB Desktop sunset). M5 has email sequences.
**What's missing:** The email sequences in M5 are generic nurture/re-engagement — they don't key off specific triggers. A marketer running trigger-based campaigns (which is the highest-converting play for MWD) has to build the connection themselves.
**Suggested fix:** Add a "Trigger-Based Outreach" section to M5 with 1-2 email angles per buying trigger from M6.

### Gap 3: No measurement framework
**What's missing:** How does the marketing team know if this toolkit is working? There are no suggested KPIs, no baseline metrics, no "measure this" guidance. For a toolkit meant to drive a GTM motion, this is a gap.
**Suggested fix:** Add a brief "How to Measure" section to the hub or as a standalone card — suggested KPIs by channel (comparison page traffic, trial signups by source, MWD-tagged pipeline) with realistic baselines.

### Gap 4: Data freshness is unclear
**Cards affected:** M8 (Top 200 data), M4/M6 (competitive pricing), M3 (customer proof stats)
**The problem:** When was the Top 200 analysis done? Are the customer screen counts (177, 159) still current? Competitive pricing changes quarterly. A toolkit meant to be used over months needs freshness indicators.
**Suggested fix:** Add "Data as of [month year]" tags to quantitative claims across cards. Add a "refresh by" note to the hub.
