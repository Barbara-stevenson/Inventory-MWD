## Outcome-First Messaging — Lead With This

Allan's rule: MWD owners care about two things. Lead with outcomes, back them with features.

### Save Time — Stop Doing the Same Work Twice

| Outcome | What Method does | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| Stop entering orders 2–3 times | Native two-way QB sync eliminates double entry between CRM and accounting | 82% of prospects cite order management chaos as their #1 pain [1] |
| End the spreadsheet shuffle | Quote-to-cash workflow keeps everything in one system — estimate, invoice, payment, work order | 42% of prospects use Excel alongside QB [1]. "We enter everything into QuickBooks and then into a spreadsheet." |
| Stop calling the office for every detail | Mobile access gives field reps real-time customer history, pricing, job details | Cigar distributor: "Driving down the road and all the information was on Method." |
| Eliminate month-end data cleanup | Changes in Method sync to QB automatically — no manual reconciliation | 79% of prospects cite visibility/reporting gaps [1] |
| Don't rebuild everything when someone leaves | Custom workflows live in Method, not in one person's head or their Excel file | "If your ops person left tomorrow, who knows the process?" — #2 discovery question from sales |

**Copy-ready outcome lines:**
- You're entering the same order three times. Method makes it once.
- The person who built your spreadsheets shouldn't be a single point of failure.
- If getting a report takes three phone calls, something's broken.

## Lines to Avoid

- "Streamline your operations" — banned word
- "Seamless integration" — banned word
- "Drive efficiency" — banned word
- "All-in-one solution" — Method isn't all-in-one for heavy manufacturing
- "Replace your ERP" — Method extends QuickBooks, not replaces ERP
- Anything that claims native inventory matches Fishbowl/Cin7/Katana depth — it doesn't
