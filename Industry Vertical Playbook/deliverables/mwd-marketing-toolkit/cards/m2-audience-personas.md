# Audience & Personas
### Card M2 | MWD Marketing Toolkit

**Use for:** Ad targeting, content calendar planning, persona-based messaging, channel selection
**Who are we talking to, what do they care about, how do we reach them.**

---

## Target Audience Summary

| Dimension | Target |
|---|---|
| Industry | Manufacturing, Wholesale & Distribution (hybrid models common) |
| Accounting system | QuickBooks Desktop or Online (primary), Xero (secondary) |
| Employee count | 2-200 (sweet spot 11-50) |
| Revenue | $1M-$25M (segments: $1-5M, $5-10M, $10-25M) |
| Geography | US and Canada (primary) |
| B2B vs. B2C | 83% B2B |
| Current tech | QuickBooks + spreadsheets + 1-2 point solutions |
| NOT our target | Over $25M revenue (likely needs enterprise ERP), pure retail, SaaS companies, already on Salesforce/NetSuite |

**In plain English:** Small-to-mid manufacturers and distributors in North America who use QuickBooks for accounting and spreadsheets for everything else. They've outgrown the spreadsheets but aren't ready to spend $20K/month on enterprise software. Revenue sweet spot is $1-25M, team size 2-200 employees.

## Three Personas Marketing Should Know

| | Owner / GM (Decision Maker) | Operations Manager (Validator) | Office Manager (Power User) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Who** | Built the business. Signs checks. Knows every customer and vendor. Age 40-65. | Runs day-to-day operations across locations. Manages teams, schedules, fulfillment. Age 35-55. | Enters data into QB, chases invoices, coordinates between departments. Age 30-55. |
| **Cares about** | Visibility into performance. Protecting QB investment. Not overpaying. Proof it works for businesses like theirs. | Seeing across locations without phone calls. Eliminating manual handoffs. Mobile access. Customization that matches actual workflow. | Not entering data twice. Reliable QB sync. Not looking bad if the new system breaks. Keeping things running. |
| **How they talk** | "I don't need all the bells and whistles. I need it to work with QuickBooks and show me what's happening across my locations." | "I'm the system. Everything runs through me. I need one place to see what's happening." | "If it doesn't sync with QuickBooks reliably, I'm keeping my spreadsheets." |
| **Content** | Customer stories. Price comparisons vs. enterprise. ROI framing. Short-form — they won't read a whitepaper. | Workflow demos. Before/after comparisons. Feature-specific content (quote-to-cash, dashboards, mobile). Medium-form. | How-to content. QB sync demos. Testimonials from office managers who survived the switch. Very practical. |
| **Channels** | LinkedIn, Google ("QuickBooks CRM"), Intuit Marketplace, trade shows, peer referrals | Google (operational queries), LinkedIn, industry forums, YouTube tutorials | Google (QB-specific queries), Facebook groups, QuickBooks community forums |

## Psychographic Notes for Content Creators

These aren't SaaS buyers browsing G2. Here's how they think:

| Trait | What it means for content |
|---|---|
| Built business from nothing, knows every workflow | Don't talk down to them. They know their operations better than you do. |
| Heavy Excel users | Spreadsheets are comfort zone. Acknowledge what they've built before pitching a replacement. |
| Risk-averse, skeptical of pitches | Lead with proof, not promises. Customer stories > feature lists. |
| Loyal to QuickBooks | Never suggest replacing QB. Ever. Frame Method as extending it. |
| 2-4 month evaluation cycles | Don't write for impulse buying. Nurture sequences, not one-shot CTAs. |
| Trust peers over marketing | Testimonials and referrals outperform ad copy. |
| "Show me" > "tell me" | Demos, screenshots, workflow videos beat whitepapers. |

**Tone:** Don't write like a SaaS marketing team. Write like someone who understands their business. Direct. Their language. No jargon, no buzzwords, no hype.

## Persona × Content Quick-Reference

| Persona | Awareness stage | Evaluation stage | Decision stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Owner / GM** | Thought leadership, peer stories, "do I need CRM?" | Price comparisons, customer ROI proof, M4 competitor angles | Trial CTA, DEP pitch, "keep QuickBooks" reassurance |
| **Operations Manager** | Pain-point posts, workflow demos, M7 JTBD content | Feature-specific content (quote-to-cash, multi-location), before/after | Customization depth proof (177 screens), mobile access demo |
| **Office Manager** | How-to content, QB sync explainers, "surviving the switch" | QB sync reliability proof, M3 testimonials from peers | Free trial (no credit card), support access reassurance |
