## The Problem → What They Built → The Payoff

### Production & Manufacturing Workflows

| | The Problem | What They Built in Method | The Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Luxury safe manufacturer** [Brown Safe] | 12-week production cycles tracked in spreadsheets. No visibility into which stage a job was in. Salesforce was too expensive. | Custom Production app — 177 screens tracking every stage from design through delivery. Integrated with QB Desktop + Avalara for tax + Zapier for automation. | Full production visibility without enterprise pricing. Every job tracked from quote to delivery in one system synced to QuickBooks. |
| **Medical equipment manufacturer** [Duraline] | Sales, shipping, customer care, AP, and AR all using different tools. No single system of record. | Four custom apps — Equipment tracking, Commission calculation, Purchase History, Email Status. Invoice grids with bulk printing. Custom fields syncing to QBO. | 15 users across every department on one system. Method became their operating system, not just their CRM. |

### Franchise & Multi-Location Operations

| | The Problem | What They Built in Method | The Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mobility equipment franchise** [Mobility City] | 54 franchise locations each doing their own thing. No standardized process. No way to see who was following the playbook. | Custom sales receipt system enforcing standardized product names across all locations. Compliance tracking measuring adoption per franchise. Custom security controls per location. Service and rental management with required fields. 159 screens total. | One CRM running 54 locations with measurable compliance. Scaling plan to 150–200 franchises on the same system. |

### Field Sales & Mobile Operations

| | The Problem | What They Built in Method | The Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cigar & wine distributor** [Vintage Makers] | Field reps across 6 states with 3,400 SKUs. No access to customer history or pricing on the road. QuickBooks can't process tobacco payments. | Tablet-based field sales — full customer history, previous purchases, live pricing. Custom photo galleries tied to inventory showing real installations. Custom tax logic for Massachusetts 40% excise tax + state-by-state tax-on-tax. Authorize.net payment processing. | Reps close on the road with full context. 2–2.5 year sales cycles tracked with complete design history preserved. "I have yet to find anything that I can't get customized." |
| **$25M food distributor** [Europena] | Two mirrored companies. Store audits done on paper. No way to capture leads at trade shows. QB Desktop reports required manual export to get operational data. | iPad store-level audit app with photos and notes. Web to Lead integration for trade show signups. Custom rolling data apps for operational reporting. All running across two mirrored entities. | Considered switching CRMs — stayed because the customization investment was too deep to walk away from. |

### Inventory Solutions (Built, Not Bought)

**⚠️ Reminder: Method does NOT have native inventory management. These are customization stories.**

| | The Problem | What They Built in Method | The Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Power tools distributor** [Techtronic/Milwaukee] | Needed inventory tracking inside the CRM — serial numbers, bin locations, multi-warehouse. Didn't want a separate system. | Full custom in-Method inventory: serial number tracking, bin location management, multi-warehouse visibility, back order management. All built through Method's customization platform. | Complete inventory control inside the CRM without a third-party tool. Every warehouse, every serial number, every bin — one system. |
| **Electronics company** [Saelig] | Needed deep inventory (SOS Inventory) but also needed CRM workflows in Method. Two systems that didn't talk to each other. | API integration: Method → SOS Inventory → QBO circular sync. Custom API app routines. View-by-warehouse button for operational visibility. Automated 3x daily sync. | Inventory lives in SOS (where it belongs), CRM lives in Method (where it belongs), and they sync automatically three times a day. |
| **Engine parts distributor** [Remanufactured.com] | CRM pipeline in Method, inventory and sales orders in SOS Inventory. Needed clean data flow without overlap. | Clean separation: Method for CRM pipeline, SOS for all sales orders and inventory. 2-hour scheduled sync. Manual "Update from SOS" button for real-time checks. | Each system does what it's best at. No data conflicts, no double entry, clear ownership. |
