## The Big Picture: Method's Position

Method sits in a gap nobody else fills — operational CRM for QuickBooks-based manufacturers and distributors. Enterprise tools (NetSuite, Salesforce) serve this market but at 5-10x the cost. Horizontal CRMs (HubSpot, Zoho) serve SMBs but can't do MWD operations. Open-source (Odoo) can do everything but wants you off QuickBooks.

**The positioning statement for all competitive content:**
"For manufacturers and distributors who run on QuickBooks, Method is the only CRM with native two-way QB sync and the customization depth to match how your business actually works — without the enterprise price tag."

## vs. NetSuite

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Method is the NetSuite alternative for companies that aren't ready to leave QuickBooks or spend enterprise money. |
| **Headlines** | "NetSuite quoted you $30K? Here's what $600/month gets you." · "Keep QuickBooks. Get the operational CRM you actually need." · "The manufacturer's alternative to NetSuite — without the 6-month implementation." |

| **Customer proof** | A cigar distribution company rejected NetSuite as "too heavy and expensive." |

## vs. Salesforce

| | |

## vs. HubSpot

| | |
|---|---|

## vs. Zoho

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Zoho looks cheap until you stack the apps you actually need for MWD. |
| **Headlines** | "Zoho CRM starts at $14/user. Then you add inventory. Then accounting sync. Then..." · "The real cost of Zoho for manufacturing: ~$168/user when you add what you need." · "Zero QuickBooks Desktop sync. That's what Zoho offers QBDT users." |
| **Key stat** | Zero QBDT integration. QBO sync is third-party only ($50-$120/month extra). Full MWD stack ~$168/user vs. Method at $27-$73/user with QB sync included. |
| **Content approach** | Zoho's headline price is attractive. The angle is total cost of ownership for MWD. CRM alone isn't enough — you need inventory, QB sync, project management, customer portal. Stack those Zoho products and the price changes fast. On QB Desktop? Zoho doesn't sync at all. |

## vs. Odoo

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Odoo can do everything. But keeping QuickBooks requires a third-party connector — and Odoo really wants you on their accounting instead. |
| **Headlines** | "Odoo wants you off QuickBooks. Method was built for it." · "Open source doesn't mean free when you need QB integration." · "Odoo's MRP is stronger. Method's QuickBooks sync is native. Pick your priority." |
| **Key stat** | Odoo's QB connector is third-party ($49-$200/month) with sync limitations. Method's is native two-way. |
| **Content approach** | Give Odoo credit — their MFG and inventory modules are strong. The angle: if QuickBooks is your accounting system and you plan to keep it, Method is built for that and Odoo isn't. For prospects willing to leave QB, Odoo is a real competitor. For QB loyalists, Method wins. |
| **Be honest** | Odoo's manufacturing capabilities (MRP, BOM, shop floor) are deeper than Method's. If heavy MFG is the need and QB isn't sacred, acknowledge that. |

## vs. Microsoft Dynamics 365

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Dynamics is enterprise complexity and cost for companies that usually aren't ready for either. |
| **Headlines** | "Dynamics 365 is built for enterprises with dedicated IT teams. You have 30 employees and a QuickBooks file." · "Microsoft wants you in their ecosystem. Method works with the one you already have." |
| **Key stat** | Business Central: $70/user/month. Add Sales ($65/user) + Field Service ($95/user) and costs scale fast. Implementation: $20K-$100K+. |
| **Content approach** | Most MWD businesses evaluating Dynamics are doing so because a consultant recommended it or they assume they need "the Microsoft stack." If QB works for accounting and you need operational CRM, you don't need to replace your tech stack. Method extends QB. Dynamics replaces it. |
| **Be honest** | Dynamics has strong MFG modules (production orders, warehouse management, supply chain). If 100+ employees, $25M+ revenue, and ready to leave QB, Dynamics may be the right move. |

## vs. Monday.com

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Monday is a project management tool pretending to be a CRM. It doesn't know what a QuickBooks invoice is. |

| **Key stat** | Monday CRM: $12/seat/month (3-seat min). No native QB sync — third-party connectors ($50-$150/month) sync contacts only, not transactions. |

But no quote-to-cash flow, no native QB sync, no transaction management. For project tracking, Monday is fine. For operational CRM with accounting integration, it's a spreadsheet with better colors. |
| **Be honest** | Monday's UI is genuinely better-looking than Method's. If primary need is visual project tracking and light contact management, Monday wins on UX. Method wins when work goes deeper. |

## vs. Thryv

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | Thryv is built for service businesses that want scheduling and marketing in one box. Not for MWD operational workflows and real QuickBooks sync. |
| **Headlines** | "Thryv syncs with QuickBooks every 12 hours. Method syncs in real time." · "Scheduling and social media won't help you manage 3,400 SKUs across 6 states." · "$244/month per location. That's Thryv's entry price — per location, not per user." |
| **Key stat** | ~$244/location/month + $250 onboarding. QB sync: 12hrs, one-way (QBO→Thryv), limited data. QBDT integration discontinued for new users. Method: $27-$73/user/month, real-time two-way. |
| **Be honest** | Thryv's scheduling, social media, and marketing automation are features Method doesn't have. For a service business wanting booking + payments + communication in one tool, Thryv is simpler. Method wins on operational depth, QB sync, and customization. |

**Key QB sync differences (for comparison content):**

| Dimension | Method | Thryv |
|---|---|---|
| Integration type | Native, built-in | In-app (QBO) / Discontinued (QBDT) |
| Sync frequency | Real-time, instant | Every 12 hrs (QBO→Thryv direction) |
| Data synced | Customers, invoices, items, payments, estimates, sales orders, custom fields, refunds | Customers, invoices, payments, estimates only |
| QB Desktop support | Full native sync | Discontinued for new users |
| Email required to sync | No | Yes |
| Custom field sync | Yes | No |
| Pricing model | Per user ($27-$73) | Per location (~$244+) |

**Why this matters for MWD:** 78% of Method's top 200 MWD accounts are on QuickBooks Desktop. Thryv has discontinued QBDT integration for new users. That alone disqualifies Thryv for the majority of the MWD target market.

## vs. "QuickBooks + Spreadsheets" (The Real Competitor)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Angle** | The status quo is the biggest competitor. Most MWD businesses aren't evaluating CRMs — they're deciding whether to change at all. 71% run QB only, another 15% run QB + spreadsheets. [1] |
| **Headlines** | "Your spreadsheets are getting too complex for anyone else to use." · "QuickBooks handles accounting. Who handles everything else?" · "What happens when the person who built your spreadsheets leaves?" |
| **Key stat** | 86% of MWD prospects run QB alone or QB + spreadsheets. Enterprise CRMs combined are under 7% of the competitive set. [1] |
| **Content approach** | Don't frame it as "spreadsheets are bad." Frame it as "you've outgrown them and you know it." Trigger words: key person leaves, added a location, quoting errors, can't see what's happening. Validate the pain first, then show what's possible. |

## SEO Page Structure Recommendation

For each competitor comparison page, use this structure:
1. One-paragraph summary of both platforms
2. Side-by-side pricing table
3. Feature comparison table (5-7 dimensions that matter for MWD)
4. "When to choose [Competitor]" — be honest
5. "When to choose Method" — lead with QB sync and operational workflows
6. Customer proof point
7. CTA: Free 10-day trial
