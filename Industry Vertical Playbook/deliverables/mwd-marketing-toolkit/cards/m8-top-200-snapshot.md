# Who's Actually Using Method? — The Top 200 Snapshot
### Card M8 | MWD Marketing Toolkit

**Use for:** Getting a feel for who our best MWD customers are, content targeting, ad audience building, lookalike modeling
**These are Method's top 200 MWD accounts by SAAS lifetime value. This is who we're building for.**

---

## The Quick Version

If you're in a rush, here's the Method MWD customer in one sentence:

**A 15-person B2B manufacturer or distributor in the US making $4.4M/year, running QuickBooks Desktop, with about 8 Method users and 40 custom screens built over the past 6 years.**

That's the median. Now let's break it down.

---

## By the Numbers

### How Big Are They?

| Employees | % of Top 200 | What this tells us |
|---|---|---|
| 1-10 | 41% | Scrappy teams — often the owner IS the operations manager |
| 11-50 | 43% | The sweet spot — big enough to need systems, small enough to hate enterprise pricing |
| 51-200 | 15% | Larger mid-market — multi-department, multi-location |
| 201-500 | 1% | Rare but real — usually the most customized accounts |

**Median employee count: 14.** These aren't tiny startups. They're established businesses with real operations. But they're not Fortune 500 either.

### How Much Revenue?

| Revenue Range | % of Top 200 | |
|---|---|---|
| Under $1M | 8% | Early-stage or very lean operations |
| $1-5M | 43% | The biggest segment — our bread and butter |
| $5-10M | 23% | Growing operations, often multi-location |
| $10-25M | 21% | Serious mid-market — could go enterprise but don't |
| $25M+ | 5% | The outliers — prove Method scales |

**Median revenue: $4.4M.** Nearly two-thirds fall between $1-10M. This is the "too big for spreadsheets, too smart for enterprise pricing" zone.

### What Do They Make or Move?

| Vertical | Count | % |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacturing | 101 | 51% |
| Wholesale & Distribution | 71 | 35% |
| Retail (with MWD ops) | 28 | 14% |

**Manufacturing leads**, but distribution is close behind. And many are hybrids — they manufacture AND distribute, or distribute AND do field service. Retail accounts are typically retailers with wholesale or distribution arms.

**Top industry keywords** across the 200: industrial equipment (12), furniture & furnishings (8), medical devices/supplies (7), packaging (6), construction (5), apparel (5), wine & spirits (5), chemicals (3), automotive (3).

### Where Are They?

| Region | % |
|---|---|
| US | 90% |
| Canada | 8% |
| UK / Other | 2% |

Almost entirely North America. This is our market.

## The QuickBooks Story

| QuickBooks Version | % of Top 200 |
|---|---|
| QB Desktop | 78% |
| QB Online | 22% |

**78% are still on QuickBooks Desktop.** This is a huge marketing insight. Our QBDT sync is rare among CRMs — most competitors only sync with QBO if they sync at all. This is a moat.

## How Deep Does Method Go?

### Customization Depth

| Total Custom Screens | # of Accounts | |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 5 | Brand new or uncustomized |
| 1-10 | 23 | Light customization |
| 11-50 | 88 | Standard — most accounts land here |
| 51-100 | 51 | Serious investment in Method |
| 100+ | 33 | Power users — Method IS their operating system |

**Average: 68 total custom screens.** The median is 39. But the top tier (100+ screens) includes accounts that have essentially built their entire business operations inside Method.

### How Many People Use It?

**Average: 10 users per account. Median: 8. Max: 74.**

Most accounts have 5-15 users — which at $27-73/user means monthly Method bills of $135-$1,095. Affordable enough that price isn't the issue. Value is.

## Product Complexity

| Level | Manufacturing | Distribution | Retail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Very High | 52 | 16 | 5 |
| High | 34 | 36 | 11 |
| Medium-High | 7 | 11 | 4 |

**86% of manufacturers have High or Very High product complexity.** These are businesses with complex SKU catalogs, custom configurations, multi-stage production, and intricate order fulfillment requirements. This complexity is WHY they need Method — simple CRMs can't handle their workflows.

## So What Does This Mean for Marketing?

**Your ideal prospect looks like this:**
- 11-50 employees (43% of top 200)
- $1-10M revenue (66% of top 200)
- Manufacturer or distributor (86% of top 200)
- On QuickBooks Desktop (78%) or Online (22%)
- In the US (90%)
- Selling B2B (83% based on full MWD analysis)
- High product complexity (77% of top 200)

**Channels that make sense:** LinkedIn (where these decision-makers are), Google search (QuickBooks + CRM keywords), Intuit marketplace (where QB users shop), industry trade shows, peer referrals.

**The message that lands:** "You've outgrown spreadsheets but you're not ready for NetSuite. Method sits right in that gap — and it actually works with your QuickBooks."
