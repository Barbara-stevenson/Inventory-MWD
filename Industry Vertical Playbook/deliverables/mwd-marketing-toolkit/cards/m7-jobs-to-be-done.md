# Jobs to Be Done
### Card M7 | MWD Marketing Toolkit

**Use for:** Campaign messaging, feature marketing, content strategy, sales enablement
**The actual workflows MWD customers are hiring Method to do — validated by prospect conversations and customer data.**

---

## The Core Job

> **"Give me operational control of my business without replacing the accounting system I already trust."**

Every MWD customer who chose Method is extending QuickBooks into areas QB was never designed for: quoting, order tracking, production management, field operations, multi-location visibility.

---

## How to Read This Card

Jobs are grouped by **buyer intent**, not alphabetically:

- **Getting Started** — High prospect demand. These are the problems that get people in the door. ¹
- **Going Deeper** — Medium demand. This is where Method starts to separate from basic CRM tools. ¹ ²
- **Full Commitment** — Lower prospect volume, highest customer depth. These are what our stickiest accounts built. ²

Each job has three layers: the **hook** (how prospects describe the problem), the **solve** (what Method does), and the **power move** (what our best customers built with it).

### Job 1: Quote-to-Cash Without the Spreadsheet

**Pillar:** Yes to Quote-to-Cash · **Headline:** "From quote to cash without the spreadsheet in between."

> **Prospect voice:** *"What Alan is going to quote the customer is not our cost on the purchase order that I send to the vendor, because we quote them, not our cost… You know, we need to make a profit off it."* ¹
> — MWD prospect, discovery call

```
Inquiry → Estimate → Sales Order → Pick/Pack → Ship → Invoice → Payment
   ↕          ↕          ↕           ↕         ↕        ↕         ↕
  CRM     QuickBooks  QuickBooks   Method    Method  QuickBooks  Portal
               ↕↕↕ ALL SYNCED — ONE ENTRY ↕↕↕
```

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pain | Over half of MWD prospects cite order management as their biggest operational pain — quoting, invoicing, fulfillment tracking across disconnected tools. ¹ |
| Method solves it | One connected flow from first contact to collected payment, all syncing back to QuickBooks. No re-entering. Once the flow is built, it doesn't leave when your quote person does. |
| Power move | Nearly every top-200 customer uses some version of this flow. Quoting is the #1 feature request across MWD prospects (40%). ¹ ² |

### Job 3: Customer History in Field Reps' Hands

**Pillar:** Yes to Real-World Business · **Headline:** "Your field reps don't need to call the office anymore."

> **Prospect voice:** *"Well, because most of the time, it's either if I get a phone call or I get an email, hey, where are we on this? I have to get up and go walk, find it, see where we're at, and then walk back and answer."* ¹
> — MWD prospect, discovery call

```
Field Rep (tablet/phone)
    │
    ├─→ Opens Method → Full customer history, pricing, past orders
    │
    ├─→ Creates quote on-site
    │
    ├─→ Quote syncs to QB automatically
    │
    └─→ Office team follows up — no phone call needed
```

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pain | Distribution reps and field teams need info on the road. Calling the office for pricing/history costs deals. 9% of prospects specifically request mobile access. ¹ |
| Method solves it | Mobile-accessible CRM with full customer context. Field reps close on the road with real data. |
| Power move | One distributor's reps run entire workflow from tablets across 6 states. A food distributor uses iPads for store-level audits. ² |

Order Received → Work Order Created → Stage 1 → Stage 2 → ... → Quality Check → Ship Ready → Invoice

                        └──── Custom screens track every stage ────────┘

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pain | Manufacturers with custom products (12-week cycles, multi-stage, made-to-order) need pipeline visibility. QB can't do this. |
