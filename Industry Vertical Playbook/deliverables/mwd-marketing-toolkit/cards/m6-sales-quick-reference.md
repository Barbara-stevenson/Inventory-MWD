## Product Overview (What Marketing Needs to Know)

**Method CRM** is a Customizable CRM for QuickBooks-based SMBs. It's sold as packs and apps — packs group related functionality, apps are the customizable interfaces inside each pack.

**Every user gets:**
- Contact Management Pack (required)
- Accounting Lists Pack with QuickBooks sync (free with every account)

**Other product lines to be aware of:**
- **Method Field Services** — work orders, dispatching, mobile field work, invoicing, QB sync
- **Method Proposals** — proposal creation, approvals, e-signatures, automated invoicing, recurring billing

For MWD marketing, focus on **Method CRM** as the core product. Field Services is relevant for MWD companies with service teams, but it has its own marketing motion.

**Key pricing facts for content:**
- Free 10-day trial, no credit card required

## Inventory — What to Claim and What Not To

| ✓ Say this | ✗ Don't say this |
|---|---|
| Method is customizable enough that customers build inventory solutions inside it | Method has native or built-in inventory management (it doesn't) |
| Customers have built multi-warehouse tracking, serial numbers, bin locations, back order management using Method's customization platform | Method replaces dedicated inventory tools like Fishbowl, Cin7, or Katana |
| Method integrates with third-party tools like SOS Inventory via API | Method handles BOM, kitting, lot tracking, or shop floor control |
| The customization story IS the inventory story — proof of how far Method bends | — |

**The honest framing:** Method doesn't ship with an inventory module. But it's customizable enough that real customers have built full inventory workflows inside it — and others integrate dedicated tools via API. Lead with the customization story, not a feature claim.
