MWD Positioning Brief - Method CRM
Method is the Customizable CRM for manufacturers and distributors who run on QuickBooks. They've outgrown spreadsheets but aren't ready for enterprise ERP. Method gives them operational control - quoting, order management, inventory visibility, mobile field access - without ripping out the accounting system they depend on.
March 2026  |  Based on 465 prospect calls, 6 customer interviews, 200 top accounts, 1,019 MWD accounts
The Customer

They run a $1-25M manufacturing or distribution company with 11-50 employees. QuickBooks is their accounting system and they have no intention of replacing it. Everything else - quoting, orders, customer management, inventory tracking - lives in spreadsheets, someone's inbox, or someone's head.

They know the spreadsheets aren't working anymore. Orders get entered twice. The field team calls the office for every price check. Month-end is a nightmare. But they've seen what enterprise software costs and what the implementation looks like, and they're not doing that.

They're not browsing G2 for CRMs. They're Googling "QuickBooks CRM" or "how to manage quotes in QuickBooks" because they want to fix a specific operational pain without blowing up what's already working. 82% cite order management chaos as their #1 pain. 93% run QuickBooks.

Competitive Landscape
1
Status Quo: QB + Spreadsheets (86% of prospects)
The real competitor. They're not comparing CRMs - they're deciding whether to change at all. Method wins by validating the pain first, then showing what's possible without leaving QuickBooks.
2
Mid-Market: Zoho, Thryv, Monday, Odoo, HubSpot
None have native QB Desktop sync. Zoho and Monday need third-party connectors. Thryv discontinued QBDT support. HubSpot stops at the sale. Odoo wants you off QuickBooks entirely. Method wins on QB sync + operational depth.
3
Enterprise: NetSuite, Salesforce, Dynamics 365 (<7% of prospects)
Right tools for $25M+ companies ready to leave QB. Method wins everyone else on cost (5-10x cheaper), QB sync, speed to value, and no 6-month implementation.

Always Get Right / Never Say
Always Get Right
"Customizable CRM" is the category. Not custom. Not customized.
QB sync is native and two-way. Every competitor needs a connector or can't sync at all.
Inventory is handled through customization, not a native module. Lead with the customization story.
The real competitor is inertia. 86% of prospects run QB + spreadsheets. Not other CRMs.
Never suggest replacing QuickBooks. Frame Method as extending it.
Never Say
"Method has inventory management." It doesn't natively. It's a customization + integration story.
"We're cheaper than NetSuite." Say right-sized, not cheaper.
"Replace your spreadsheets." Say you've outgrown them.
"Full ERP." Method is not an ERP. It's an operational CRM with ERP-adjacent capabilities.
Don't trash competitors. Acknowledge what they do well. We win on fit, not on FUD.
