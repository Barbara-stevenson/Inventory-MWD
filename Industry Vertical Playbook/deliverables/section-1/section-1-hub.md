## Key Principles Across Section 1

**Built from Method's customer base, verified against industry:** Every insight in this section starts with what real Method MWD customers actually experience — structured interviews, analysis of 1,019 classified MWD accounts, and detailed examination of Method's top 200 MWD customers. Industry data and competitive intelligence validate and contextualize those findings. Customer insights first. Industry verification second.

**MWD focus:** This playbook targets Manufacturing and Wholesale/Distribution businesses. Method's top 200 MWD accounts break down as 101 manufacturing and 71 wholesale/distribution. Many operate hybrid models that blend both. Companies with incidental service components (like Duraline's repair/refurbishment) are included, but pure field service businesses are out of scope.

**Inventory positioning:** Method's inventory story is QB sync plus customization — not native inventory management. Two proven paths for customers who need more: third-party API integration (e.g., SOS Inventory) or in-house custom builds inside Method. Be upfront about this. See [01d Competitive Positioning](cards/01d-competitive-positioning.md) for the honest gaps section.

**The real competitor is inertia:** Most MWD businesses aren't evaluating CRMs. They're evaluating whether to change at all. "QB + spreadsheets" is the status quo to beat. See [03 JTBD Analysis](cards/03-jtbd-analysis.md) for the habits of the present.

**Customization is the moat:** Retention doesn't come from features — it comes from customization investment. Accounts with 50+ custom screens don't switch. See [02 Customer Interview Insights](cards/02-customer-interview-insights.md), Theme 1.

**Three personas, one buying process:** The Owner decides, the Ops Manager validates, and the Office Manager determines long-term adoption. Engage all three. See [04b](cards/04b-persona-ops-manager.md), [04c](cards/04c-persona-owner-gm.md), [04d](cards/04d-persona-office-admin.md).
