## Six Forces Pushing MWD Businesses Toward Method

### 1. QB Desktop Sunset Is Coming
Intuit hasn't announced a date, but QB Desktop discontinuation is widely expected. MWD businesses — especially manufacturers — are heavy Desktop users and resist migration because QBO has fewer features. Method acts as a transition bridge: the operational layer makes QBO less painful.
> Brown Safe: "If it's working, don't fix it." Duraline: "QuickBooks Desktop was one of the best features… QuickBooks Online is horrible." Three of six interviewed MWD customers are Desktop-attached.

### 2. Multi-Location Complexity Is Growing
Manufacturing and distribution are inherently multi-site. QB handles accounting but not operational coordination. Enterprise tools (NetSuite, Cin7) solve this but cost $5K-$20K/month and take 6-12 months.
> Mobility City manages 54 franchise locations. Vintage Makers operates across 6 states. Europena runs two mirrored companies. Multi-location is the norm, not the exception.

### 3. Customization Demand Is Rising
Every MWD business has unique terminology and workflows. Pre-built solutions are too rigid. Method's pay-per-use customization and DEP ($325/month) hit this directly.
> Brown Safe: 177 custom screens. Mobility City: 159 custom screens. Europena: "The greatest thing is… we are able to customize." Customization is the #1 reason for choosing — and staying with — Method.

### 4. Mobile and Field Access Are Table Stakes
MWD businesses operate beyond the office — sales reps visiting accounts, warehouse teams checking stock, delivery crews confirming orders. Real-time mobile access to customer data, inventory, and order status is expected, not optional.
> Vintage Makers: "Driving down the road and all the information was on Method." Europena built store-level audit workflows on iPads with photos and notes. Mobile access shows up in every MWD use case.

### 5. System Fragmentation Is the #1 Pain
MWD businesses typically run 6-10 point solutions. Manual data entry between systems creates errors, delays, and zero visibility. 70% of SMBs cite lack of funds as the barrier to fixing this.
> Vintage Makers pre-Method: "We were using spreadsheets and VLOOKUPs." Mobility City: "Each location was doing their own thing." The pattern is universal.

### 6. Integration Ecosystem Keeps Expanding
More tools (Avalara, Authorize.net, Zapier, Fishbowl) means more need for a hub connecting everything back to QB. Method's native QB sync plus Zapier/API integration positions it as that hub.
> Brown Safe: QB + Avalara + Zapier. Europena: QB + Fishbowl (switching to Markov) + Method. Vintage Makers: QB Desktop Enterprise + Method + state tax compliance tools.
