## Where Method Fits

Method occupies a unique position: **the only CRM with deep two-way QuickBooks sync that also handles operational workflows for MWD businesses at SMB pricing.**

---

## Competition Comes from Four Directions

| Category | Examples | Their strength | Their weakness vs. Method | When we lose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise ERP** | NetSuite, Dynamics, SAP | Full ERP + CRM + manufacturing | $5K-$20K+/mo, 6-12 month implementations, no practical QB sync | Almost never at our ICP size — they self-disqualify on cost |
| **Traditional CRM** | Salesforce, HubSpot | Strong sales/marketing CRM | No inventory, no production, no work orders, no QB sync | Buyer is sales-heavy with minimal operational needs |
| **Open-Source ERP** | Odoo, Creatio | Flexible with MRP modules | QB integration needs third-party connectors, high implementation complexity | Buyer has technical resources and is willing to leave QB |
| **Specialized Tools** | Fishbowl, Katana, inFlow | Deep inventory / MRP | Not CRM tools — they complement Method, not compete with it | See "Partner Ecosystem" below |

## Partner Ecosystem: Specialized Tools Work *With* Method

Specialized inventory and manufacturing tools (Fishbowl, SOS Inventory, Katana, inFlow) aren't competitors — they're **complements.** Several Method customers run both:

- **Europena** uses Fishbowl for deep inventory management alongside Method for CRM, custom audits, and field workflows
- **SLE Technologies** syncs Method → SOS Inventory → QBO in a circular workflow with API app routines and warehouse-level views
- **Cypress Engine** uses Method for CRM pipeline + SOS Inventory for all sales orders and inventory, with scheduled sync between systems
- **Custom builds inside Method** — customers like Euroboor have built full warehouse tracking with serial numbers, bin locations, multi-warehouse support, and back-order management entirely within Method

**The sales conversation:** "Do you need deep MRP with BOM, kitting, and lot tracking? If so, a tool like Fishbowl or SOS Inventory handles that — and Method integrates with it for your CRM and operational workflows. If you need inventory visibility and warehouse tracking? We can build that inside Method."

## Appendix: Honest Gaps

Be upfront about these. Honesty builds trust with risk-averse MWD buyers.

- **Inventory is QB sync, not native.** No standalone inventory management system. Complex needs require third-party tools (via API) or custom builds inside Method.
- **No native BOM, kitting, lot tracking, or shop floor control.** Heavy manufacturers need a dedicated tool alongside Method.
- **QBO users lose significant functionality** vs. QB Desktop (no assemblies, sales orders, advanced inventory, price levels).
- **No native e-commerce integration.**
- **EDI / large-scale order automation is limited.**
- **Email marketing throttles** beyond ~3K contacts — layer in SendGrid or Mailchimp via Zapier.
- **Reporting requires customization** for MWD-specific dashboards.
- **Mobile isn't warehouse-optimized** out of the box — warehouse workflows (pick/pack/ship, bin scanning) need customization.
- **Performance at scale** — heavily customized accounts may experience slowdowns.
