# MWD Ideal Customer Profile
### Card 04a | Section 1: Market & Customer Foundation

**Audience:** Sales reps (qualification), marketing (targeting), leadership (resource allocation)

## The ICP in One Sentence

A QuickBooks-using manufacturer or distributor with 10-50 employees and $1M-$250M revenue that has outgrown spreadsheets but isn't ready for enterprise ERP.

---

## Firmographic Criteria

| Dimension | Ideal | Acceptable | Disqualifying |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Accounting system** | QuickBooks Desktop or Online | Xero | No QB or Xero |
| **Employee count** | 10-50 | 5-10 or 50-100 | Under 5 or 100+ |
| **Revenue** | $2M-$50M | $1M-$2M or $50M-$250M | Under $1M |
| **Geography** | US, Canada | Australia, UK | Non-English markets |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing, Wholesale & Distribution | Hybrid models | Pure retail, SaaS, professional services, field service |
| **Locations** | 2-10 sites | 1 site with growth plans, or 10-50 (franchise) | Single location, no growth |
| **Current tools** | QB + spreadsheets + 1-2 point solutions | QB + basic CRM | Already on Salesforce, NetSuite, or full ERP |

---

## Quick Qualification Checklist

Use this in the first 2 minutes of a discovery call.

- [ ] Do they use QuickBooks? (If no → likely disqualified)
- [ ] Do they have 10+ employees? (If under 5 → probably too small)
- [ ] Do they manufacture, distribute, or warehouse? (If pure retail, field service, or SaaS → wrong vertical)
- [ ] Are they using spreadsheets alongside QB for operational tracking? (If yes → strong signal)
- [ ] Have they evaluated or rejected enterprise tools? (If yes → very strong signal)

Three "yes" answers = strong ICP fit. Move to full discovery.

## Appendix: Behavioral Signals & Psychographic Profile

### Buying Readiness Signals

**Strong signals (prioritize these leads):**
- Searching for "QuickBooks CRM" or "CRM for manufacturing" on Google or Intuit App Marketplace
- Currently using QB + spreadsheets for inventory tracking, quoting, or job scheduling
- Recently added a location or franchise and struggling with visibility
- Evaluated enterprise ERP (NetSuite, SAP, Dynamics) and rejected it on cost or complexity
- Actively hiring operations or office management roles (indicates process growing pains)

**Moderate signals (worth pursuing):**
- Using QB Desktop and concerned about sunset timeline
- Using a basic CRM (HubSpot free, Zoho) but needs operational workflows
- Has field teams without mobile tools
- Manually tracks work orders, production, or service jobs

**Weak signals (nurture, don't prioritize):**
- Just started the business (under 2 years old, under $1M)
- Happy with current spreadsheet setup and not experiencing pain
- Evaluating pure CRM without operational needs

### Psychographic Profile

MWD buyers share a mindset distinct from typical SaaS buyers.

**They are:** Old-school entrepreneurs who built their businesses from nothing. Hands-on operators who know every workflow personally. Risk-averse, cautious about new tech, skeptical of sales pitches. Heavy Excel users who've made spreadsheets do things spreadsheets shouldn't do. Loyal to QuickBooks. Practical — they want to see it work before they believe it.

**They are NOT:** Early adopters excited about new software. Delegators who hand tech decisions to IT teams. Feature-checklist buyers. Impulse buyers — expect 2-4 month sales cycles.

**The winning approach:** Show, don't tell. Let them build during the trial. Reference customers like them. Be honest about gaps. Earn trust through transparency.

### Two MWD Sub-Segments

Method's top 200 MWD accounts: 101 manufacturing, 71 wholesale/distribution.

**Manufacturing (with Distribution):** Design, produce, and ship goods. Top sub-industries by LTV: Food & Beverage, Construction & Building, Healthcare & Medical, Industrial. Examples: Brown Safe, Duraline, Vintage Makers. Entry point: Custom production tracking, quote-to-invoice workflow, inventory visibility. Method hook: Custom Production app + QB sync + DEP partnership.

**Wholesale & Distribution:** Receive, store, allocate, sell through channels, ship. Includes food distribution, industrial supply, franchise models. Examples: Europena, Mobility City, Conequip. Entry point: Multi-location visibility, franchise standardization, order management. Method hook: Centralized dashboards + compliance tracking + multi-site QB sync + mobile access.
