## Appendix: Detailed Pain Evidence

### Fragmented Systems and Manual Data Entry
The single biggest pain. "Islands of information" — sales in one place, inventory in another, accounting in QB, field notes on paper. Every handoff is manual. Every step introduces errors. Vintage Makers: drowning in spreadsheets and VLOOKUPs. Mobility City: franchises on paper, QB, and ad-hoc processes. Duraline: uses Method across every department because fragmentation was the problem.

### Inventory Visibility
20-40% excess inventory is typical for SMBs without real-time multi-location tracking. Only 6% achieve full supply chain transparency. Method leverages QB's built-in inventory features (Inventory Parts for QBO + Desktop, Assembly Items and Advanced Inventory for Desktop only) and surfaces that data inside CRM and operational workflows. For complex needs beyond QB's native capabilities: third-party API integrations (e.g., SOS Inventory) or custom builds within Method for warehouse tracking, serial numbers, bin locations.

### QuickBooks Limitations for Manufacturing
QBO doesn't support manufacturing natively — no raw materials → finished goods conversion, no COGM tracking, negative inventory issues. But these businesses won't leave QB. Brown Safe came to Method because QB couldn't coordinate installation projects. Europena invested too heavily in QB to switch.

### Order Management and Fulfillment
The quote-to-cash-to-service chain breaks across multiple systems. Duraline runs Estimates → Sales Orders → Invoices → Work Orders → Field Crew all through Method. Brown Safe: Installation Projects is their most valuable app.

### Quoting and Pricing Complexity
Tiered pricing, volume discounts, custom configurations, tax variations across states. Vintage Makers: varying excise taxes across 6 states (Massachusetts: 40% excise tax plus tax-on-tax). Brown Safe: custom safes with 12-week production cycles.

### Reporting Blind Spots
Mobility City: "If we don't have the information, we can't behave like a national company managing 150 stores."
