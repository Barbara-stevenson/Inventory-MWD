## Seven Buying Triggers

These are the moments when "well enough" stops being enough:

1. **Adding a location** — visibility pain becomes acute overnight
2. **Losing a quote on bad margin math** — accuracy becomes urgent
3. **Key person leaves** — tribal knowledge disappears
4. **QB Desktop sunset conversation** — forces stack evaluation
5. **Failed ERP evaluation** — realizes enterprise tools are overkill
6. **Franchise/multi-site growth** — standardization becomes necessary
7. **Compliance or audit pressure** — paper processes become liability

## Appendix: Detailed Job Stories & Switching Forces

### Job 1: Operational Visibility Across Locations

**When** I'm running operations across multiple warehouses/locations and can't see inventory, orders, or job status without calling someone or checking spreadsheets, **I want to** see real-time data across all my locations in one place, **so I can** make decisions without waiting, reduce errors, and stop relying on phone calls.

- **Evidence:** Mobility City (54 franchises, needed centralized visibility). Vintage Makers (6 states, field reps need live data). Duraline (multi-location equipment tracking).

### Job 2: Quote-to-Cash Without Manual Handoffs

**When** a customer wants a quote and the process involves spreadsheets, phone calls, and manually entering the same data into QB, **I want to** go from estimate to invoice to payment to work order in one connected flow, **so I can** reduce errors, speed up fulfillment, and stop losing revenue to slow processes.

- **Evidence:** Brown Safe (12-week production cycle, everything paper-based). Europena (too many handoffs from PO to invoice). Duraline (estimates → sales orders → invoices flow).

### Job 3: Build Workflows That Match My Business

**When** off-the-shelf software forces me to change how I work instead of adapting to my process, **I want to** customize my system to match my actual terminology, steps, and rules, **so I can** keep my competitive advantage.

- **Evidence:** Europena considered switching but customization investment kept them. Vintage Makers: "I have yet to find anything that I can't get customized." Brown Safe: custom Production app for 12-week manufacturing cycle.

### Job 4: Keep QuickBooks but Get More Control

**When** QuickBooks handles my accounting fine but doesn't give me the operational workflows I need, **I want to** add operational capabilities on top of QB without replacing it, **so I can** avoid the risk, cost, and disruption of switching to a full ERP.

- **Evidence:** Every customer. Vintage Makers rejected NetSuite. Brown Safe is a heavy QBDT user. Duraline insists on keeping QB for AP.

### Job 5: Give Mobile Teams Real-Time Access

**When** my field teams are on-site without knowing customer history, order status, or inventory availability, **I want to** give them mobile access to everything they need, **so I can** close sales faster, reduce errors, and look professional.

- **Evidence:** Vintage Makers (field reps on tablets). Europena (store-level audits on iPads, Web to Lead at trade shows). Duraline (field crew with work order context).

### Switching Forces Map

**Push forces (away from status quo):**

| Force | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Spreadsheet/manual process overload | **Strong** | Brown Safe: everything paper-based. Vintage Makers: heavy VLOOKUP logic. |
| Can't see across locations | **Strong** | Mobility City: fragmented franchise data. Vintage Makers: 6-state operation. |
| Quoting/fulfillment too slow | **Strong** | Europena: too many steps. Brown Safe: 12-week cycles. |
| Current tools too rigid | **Moderate** | Europena: Fishbowl forced rigid structure. Vintage Makers: competitors couldn't adapt. |
| Enterprise tools too expensive | **Moderate** | Vintage Makers: NetSuite rejected on cost. Brown Safe: Salesforce too expensive. |

**Pull forces (toward Method):**

| Force | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| QB integration (keep what works) | **Very Strong** | Universal — every customer chose Method partly for QB sync. |
| Customization depth | **Very Strong** | 159-177 custom screens across top accounts. |
| Price vs. enterprise | **Strong** | $600-$1,200/month vs. $5K-$20K for NetSuite. |
| Mobile + operational workflows | **Strong** | Vintage Makers, Duraline, Europena all use mobile + custom ops apps. |
| DEP partnership model | **Moderate** | Duraline and Mobility City rely on ongoing DEP. |

**Switching anxieties (why buyers hesitate):**

| Anxiety | How to Address |
|---|---|
| "Will it work with our QB setup?" | Free 14-day trial. Show QB sync in first demo. |
| "How hard is implementation?" | Reference 1-2 month timelines vs. 6-12 month ERP. DEP reduces risk. |
| "Can it handle our workflows?" | Show customer examples with similar complexity. Offer DEP trial scope. |
| "What if customization doesn't work?" | Point to customers with 100+ screens still on Method years later. |
| "Our team won't adopt it" | Mobility City's compliance journey (66% → growing). Multi-champion strategy. |

**Inertia (the hardest competitor):**

| Habit | Strength |
|---|---|
| "QB + spreadsheets works well enough" | **Very Strong** — most MWD businesses aren't evaluating CRMs at all. |
| "We've always done it this way" | **Strong** — some Mobility City franchisees revert to paper. |
| "Last software change was painful" | **Moderate** — risk-averse ICP, cautious about new tech. |
