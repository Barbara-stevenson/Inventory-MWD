# Persona: Operations Manager / VP of Operations
### Card 04b | Section 1: Market & Customer Foundation

**Audience:** Sales reps (discovery prep, demo tailoring), marketing (content targeting)
**When to use:** Prospect's main contact is operations-focused — managing workflows, locations, teams

**Agent:** Riya — ICP Mapper | Research Squad

---

## Who They Are

**Title variations:** Operations Manager, VP of Operations, Director of Operations, Production Manager, Warehouse Manager, Logistics Manager

**Daily reality:** They know how every order moves from intake to delivery. They manage teams across locations, juggle schedules, track inventory, and fix problems. They live in QuickBooks and spreadsheets — switching between tabs all day. Age 35-55, often promoted from within.

---

## What Keeps Them Up at Night

| Pain | Intensity | What they say |
|---|---|---|
| Can't see across locations | **Critical** | "I have to call the warehouse to find out what we have in stock." |
| Manual handoffs between quoting and fulfillment | **Critical** | "Every order gets entered at least three times." |
| No single system connects sales → production → delivery | **High** | "I'm the system. Everything runs through me." |
| Spreadsheet dependency | **High** | "My spreadsheets are getting too complex for anyone else to use." |

---

## What Closes Them / What Loses Them

**Closes:** Seeing their actual workflow mapped in Method during trial. Customer reference from similar business. DEP pitch: "We'll build it with you." Honest acknowledgment of gaps.

**Loses:** Feature-dump demos that don't map to their process. Overpromising on inventory. Ignoring the QB relationship. Long implementation timelines without clear milestones.

## Appendix: Discovery & Demo Playbook

### Discovery Questions
- "Walk me through what happens after a customer says yes to a quote."
- "How many people touch an order before it ships?"
- "How do you track what's happening across your locations right now?"
- "What's the one report you wish you had that you can't build today?"

### Demo Focus
1. Show the quote-to-work-order flow with QB sync
2. Show a multi-location dashboard with real-time data
3. Show the Field Crew app — what a technician sees on their phone
4. Show a custom screen being built (prove customization is real)

### What They Want (But May Not Articulate)
- One place to see orders, inventory status, and team activity across locations
- Automated handoffs between steps — estimate approved → work order created → field crew assigned
- QB integration that doesn't create double entry or accounting chaos
- Custom dashboards showing their specific KPIs without asking IT
- Mobile access for field teams that actually works

### How They Evaluate Software
- Hands-on trial — they need to build something and see their actual data flowing
- Multiple stakeholders — they'll bring in owner, finance, and field leads
- Risk-averse — they've been burned before, need proof it works for businesses like theirs
- Timeline sensitivity — want to implement during a slow season

### Real Customer Examples
- **Craig at Mobility City:** Standardized 54 franchise locations. Built 159 custom screens. Drove compliance from zero to 66%.
- **Duraline Operations team:** Uses Method across sales, shipping, customer care, AP, AR.
- **Europena operations team:** Built store-level audit workflows on iPads, manages two mirrored companies, runs rolling data apps for sales tracking.
