### Key Retention Insight
Strongest retention signal isn't feature satisfaction — it's customization investment. Brown Safe (177 screens), Mobility City (159 screens), Europena all cite customization depth as why switching would be painful.

### Strategy Deep Dives

**1. Lead with integration:** Every customer interview confirms fragmentation is the core pain. Duraline uses Method across every department. Mobility City centralized 54 franchises. Vintage Makers replaced spreadsheets and VLOOKUPs.

**2. Status quo is the real competitor:** Most MWD businesses aren't evaluating CRMs — they're evaluating whether to change at all. Mobility City: "Technology alone doesn't fix bad processes."

**3. Customization as moat:** Method's pay-per-use customization separates it from every competitor category. Enterprise ERPs charge heavily for customization. Traditional CRMs are too rigid. Specialized tools can't adapt to hybrid business models.

**4. Inventory honesty:** Method's inventory story is QB sync plus customization — not native inventory management. For distribution and light manufacturing, QB sync gives visibility alongside CRM workflows. Customization extends this significantly (real customers have built warehouse tracking, serial numbers, bin locations). For heavy manufacturing, dedicated tools go deeper. Two proven paths: third-party API integration (e.g., SOS Inventory), or custom builds inside Method.

**5. DEP as wedge:** MWD buyers want someone to build it for them. $325/month for 3 hours of expert customization. Duraline: "Alex is constantly customizing." The DEP relationship mirrors how these businesses work — incremental improvement, not big-bang implementations.

**6. Operational differentiation:** Most CRM competitors don't extend into operational territory — work orders, custom quoting engines, multi-location dashboards, mobile audit tools. Method does.
