# Persona: Owner / General Manager
### Card 04c | Section 1: Market & Customer Foundation

**Audience:** Sales reps (discovery prep, executive conversations), marketing (thought leadership targeting)
**When to use:** Prospect's decision-maker is the business owner or GM — the person who signs checks and owns strategy

**Agent:** Riya — ICP Mapper | Research Squad

---

## Who They Are

**Title variations:** Owner, CEO, President, General Manager, Founder, Managing Partner

**Daily reality:** They built this business. They know every customer, every vendor, every bottleneck. They're involved in everything — from negotiating supplier terms to reviewing invoices. Technology decisions are personal because bad software costs real money. Age 40-65. Not tech-averse — tech-cautious. They've been burned before.

---

## What Keeps Them Up at Night

| Pain | Intensity | What they say |
|---|---|---|
| No visibility into overall business performance | **Critical** | "I don't know if we're profitable on this job until it's over." |
| Growing too fast for current processes | **Critical** | "We're adding locations but I can't see what's happening at each one." |
| Key-person dependency | **High** | "If Sarah leaves, nobody knows how the spreadsheets work." |
| Cost of enterprise solutions vs. actual need | **High** | "NetSuite wanted $20K/month. We're a $5M company." |
| Cash flow visibility | **High** | "I need to know what's outstanding and what's coming in." |

---

## What Closes Them / What Loses Them

**Closes:** Customer reference from a business their size, in their industry. Price comparison vs. NetSuite/Salesforce. DEP framed as "your dedicated expert, $325/month." Free trial with their own data.

**Loses:** Overselling — they can smell BS. Ignoring their QB loyalty. Long, technical demos (they want business outcomes). Hidden costs or unclear timelines.

## Appendix: Discovery & Demo Playbook

### Discovery Questions
- "What's the biggest bottleneck slowing your growth right now?"
- "How do you currently track the health of your business — what do you look at every morning?"
- "Have you looked at enterprise systems before? What happened?"
- "How many systems does your team use on a typical day?"

### Demo Focus
1. Start with the business case: "Here's how a $5M distributor uses Method" (customer story)
2. Show the executive dashboard — the view they'd see every morning
3. Show QB sync live — data flows both ways, no double entry
4. Show pricing: "$600-$1,200/month for your team size vs. what enterprise charges"

### What They Want (But May Not Articulate)
- Control without complexity — see everything, customize anything, but not manage an IT department
- Protection of their QB investment — they've spent years building their QB setup
- A partner, not a vendor — someone who understands their business and helps them build
- Proof before commitment — need to see similar businesses using Method successfully
- Predictable costs — no surprise bills, no scope creep

### How They Evaluate Software
- Gut feeling matters — they judge the sales rep as much as the software
- They ask "who else like me uses this?" — customer references are the most powerful tool
- They calculate ROI in their head — $600-$1,200/month vs. cost of one admin doing manual data entry
- They involve their operations person — owner decides, ops manager validates
- They take their time — 2-4 month sales cycle, don't rush them

### Real Customer Examples
- **Vintage Makers owner:** Founded 1994. Wine cellars + cigar distribution across 6 states. Rejected NetSuite on cost.
- **Brown Safe owner:** 40-year-old company. 12-week production cycles. Heavy QBDT user. Chose Method for customization at affordable pricing.
- **Europena leadership:** $25M food distribution running two entities. QB + customization kept them on Method.
