# Method vs. Odoo — MWD Battlecard
### Card 10b | Section 3: Competitive Intelligence

**Audience:** Sales reps, presales, leadership
**When to use:** Prospect mentions Odoo, is evaluating open-source ERP, or has technical staff exploring modular platforms

**Agent:** Dex — Battlecard Builder | Execution Squad

---

## The Numbers

| Dimension | Method | Odoo |
|---|---|---|
| Per-user pricing | $27-$73/user/month | ~$47/user/month (Enterprise, 5+ apps) |
| Implementation cost | DEP: $325/month ongoing | $5K-$50K (partner-dependent) |
| QB integration | Native two-way, real-time | **None.** Wants you on Odoo Accounting. |
| Customization | DIY + DEP expert | Developer-dependent (Python/XML) |
| Manufacturing | No native BOM/kitting/shop floor | Full MRP: BOM, routing, work orders, QC |
| Contract | Month-to-month | Annual |

---

## When Method Wins / Loses

**Wins:** Prospect keeping QuickBooks (Odoo has zero QB integration). No developers on staff. Wants fast time-to-value (1-2 mo vs. 2-6+ mo). Been burned by implementation projects. Needs CRM + operations, not full ERP.

**Loses:** Needs full MRP (Odoo's is genuinely strong at this price). Willing to leave QuickBooks. Has in-house developers. Wants single platform for everything (80+ apps).

---

## The One Question

**"Are you planning to keep QuickBooks?"** If yes — Odoo is off the table. No native QB integration, not limited, not basic — none. If they're open to switching, Odoo becomes viable. Lead with cost, speed, and DEP.

## Appendix: Talk Track & Objection Handling

### Talk Track

**Opening:** "Odoo has a strong manufacturing suite — especially for the price. The key question is what's happening with your QuickBooks. Are you planning to keep it?"

**If keeping QB:** "Odoo has no QuickBooks integration. Not limited. None. Their model is to replace your accounting with Odoo Accounting. If QB is staying, you'd need a third-party connector that adds cost and never matches native sync."

**If open to switching:** "If you're moving off QB, Odoo becomes more viable — especially for manufacturing. Method's edge is implementation speed, the DEP partnership model, and customization without developers."

**Implementation:** "Who's going to implement Odoo? Their partner ecosystem varies wildly in quality. Method's DEP is a direct relationship — $325/month, no third-party partner lottery."

### Objection Handling

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "Odoo is open-source and free" | "Community edition is free — but excludes manufacturing, multi-company, and quality management. Enterprise starts at ~$47/user, implementation runs $5K-$50K. Method's total cost is often comparable or lower, with a dedicated expert included." |
| "Odoo has manufacturing modules" | "True — Odoo's MRP is strong. The question is whether you need full MRP or CRM + operations connected to QuickBooks. If it's the latter, Method does that better and faster." |
| "We have a developer who can customize Odoo" | "That's an advantage. The question is sustainability — what happens when that developer leaves? Method's DEP means your customization expert is always there." |
| "Odoo does everything we need" | "It might. The gap is QuickBooks — Odoo doesn't connect to it. If you're moving off QB, Odoo covers a lot of ground. If QB is staying, you're looking at a third-party connector that limits what syncs." |

### Landmines

- Don't dismiss Odoo's manufacturing capabilities — they're real and strong.
- Don't ignore the free edition — address it: "Free edition excludes manufacturing, multi-company, and quality management."
- Don't claim Method is "easier to customize" — frame it as a fit question (accessible vs. deeper but technical).
