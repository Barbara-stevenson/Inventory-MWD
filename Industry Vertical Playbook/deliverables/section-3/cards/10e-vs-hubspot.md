# Method vs. HubSpot — MWD Battlecard
### Card 10e | Section 3: Competitive Intelligence

**Audience:** Sales reps, presales, BDRs
**When to use:** Prospect mentions HubSpot, is comparing CRM options, or found HubSpot through search/content marketing

**Agent:** Dex — Battlecard Builder | Execution Squad

---

## The Numbers

| Dimension | Method | HubSpot |
|---|---|---|
| CRM per-user pricing | $27-$73/user/month | Free (basic) / $20-$150/user (Sales Hub) |
| Marketing tools | Basic email (~3K contact throttle) | Strong — Marketing Hub $800-$3,600/month |
| QB sync | Native two-way, real-time | **Third-party only.** Limited, often one-way. |
| Operational workflows | Custom screens, quote-to-cash, production | Sales pipelines and deal management only |
| Manufacturing capability | Custom builds via DEP, QB inventory sync | **None.** No production, no order fulfillment. |
| Customization depth | 50-177 custom screens per customer | Custom properties and pipelines, limited beyond CRM |

---

## When Method Wins / Loses

**Wins:** Prospect needs more than sales pipeline (quote-to-cash, production, operational screens). Needs QuickBooks sync (HubSpot has none natively). Needs operational customization (177 screens at Brown Safe vs. HubSpot's property/pipeline limits). Outgrown spreadsheets and needs workflows connected to accounting.

**Loses:** Primary need is marketing (HubSpot's suite is world-class). Wants free to start (no permanent free tier at Method). Early-stage and sales-focused only. Values content/community ecosystem.

---

## The One Question

**"What happens to a deal after it closes in your CRM?"** This immediately exposes the gap between what HubSpot does (everything before the sale) and what an MWD business needs (everything before AND after — connected to QuickBooks).

## Appendix: Talk Track & Objection Handling

### Talk Track

**Opening:** "HubSpot is great for marketing and sales pipeline. The question for a manufacturer or distributor is what happens after the deal closes. How do you track the order? How does it get to QuickBooks? How do you manage production?"

**After the sale:** "Walk me through what happens after a customer says yes. Does the quote become a sales order? Who enters it into QuickBooks? Who tracks fulfillment? HubSpot handles everything before the sale. Method handles before AND after — connected to QuickBooks."

**QB question:** "HubSpot doesn't sync with QB natively. That means manual entry or a third-party connector that typically only pushes contacts — not estimates, invoices, or payments."

**Depth question:** "If you need a standard sales pipeline with custom fields, HubSpot handles that. If you need custom screens for production tracking, equipment management, multi-location operations — Method's customization goes much deeper."

### Objection Handling

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "HubSpot is free" | "The basic CRM is free and good for contact management. For MWD with QB sync, operational workflows, and production tracking, you'd need paid tiers plus third-party integrations. That's when cost comparison changes." |
| "HubSpot has great marketing tools" | "Agreed. Method focuses on operational CRM, quote-to-cash, and QB sync. Some businesses use one for marketing, another for operations. Which problem is bigger right now?" |
| "Everyone recommends HubSpot" | "Great recommendation for sales and marketing CRM. For MWD businesses on QuickBooks needing operational workflows, it's less of a fit. What does your day-to-day need beyond sales pipeline?" |
| "We just need a basic CRM" | "If that's genuinely the case, HubSpot might work. But most MWD businesses that say that actually need CRM + order management + QB sync + custom workflows. Walk me through your daily operation." |

### Landmines

- Don't compete on marketing — you'll lose. HubSpot's marketing suite is world-class.
- Don't dismiss the free tier — it's genuinely useful. The counterpoint: free CRM doesn't solve MWD operational problems.
- Don't overclaim on email marketing — Method throttles at ~3K contacts. Be honest.
- HubSpot often shows up early (SEO dominance). The conversation shifts once you get into operational requirements. Don't fight the brand — redirect to operational fit.
