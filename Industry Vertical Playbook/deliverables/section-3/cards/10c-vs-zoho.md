# Method vs. Zoho — MWD Battlecard
### Card 10c | Section 3: Competitive Intelligence

**Audience:** Sales reps, presales, leadership
**When to use:** Prospect mentions Zoho, is comparing "all-in-one" platforms, or is attracted to breadth and pricing

**Agent:** Dex — Battlecard Builder | Execution Squad

---

## The Numbers

| Dimension | Method | Zoho (Full MWD Stack) |
|---|---|---|
| CRM per-user pricing | $27-$73/user/month | $14-$65/user/month (CRM only) |
| Full MWD stack cost | CRM pricing + DEP optional | ~$168/user/month (CRM + Inventory + Books + Projects) |
| QuickBooks Desktop sync | Native two-way, real-time | **No native QBDT integration.** |
| QuickBooks Online sync | Native two-way, real-time | Limited via Zoho Books or third-party |
| Customer portal | Included free | $125/month add-on |
| Customization | DIY + DEP ($325/month) | Admin-configurable, Deluge scripting for advanced |
| Contract | Month-to-month | Annual (discounted) or monthly |

---

## When Method Wins / Loses

**Wins:** Prospect uses QuickBooks Desktop (Zoho has zero QBDT integration). Real cost adds up (~$168/user for full MWD stack vs. $73 Method). Needs deep operational customization (177 screens at Brown Safe). Needs customer portal (free vs. $125/mo). Wants one expert, not a suite to manage.

**Loses:** Wants everything under one brand (45+ Zoho apps). Already on Zoho Books. Needs strong marketing automation (Method throttles at ~3K contacts). Prioritizes self-service setup.

---

## The One Question

**"What's your total cost when you add up all the Zoho apps you'd actually need?"** CRM looks cheap at $14/user. Stack CRM + Inventory + Books + Projects for MWD and you're at ~$168/user — more than double Method's Enterprise tier.

## Appendix: Talk Track & Objection Handling

### Talk Track

**Opening:** "Zoho has a big ecosystem — lots of apps under one roof. Two things to figure out: What QuickBooks version are you on, and what does the total cost look like when you add up all the Zoho apps you'd actually need?"

**QBDT question:** "If you're on QuickBooks Desktop, Zoho doesn't connect to it natively. You'd need a third-party tool that adds cost and never syncs as cleanly as native integration."

**Real cost:** "Zoho CRM starts at $14/user — that's the number that catches your eye. But MWD needs CRM + inventory + accounting integration + project tracking. Stack those and you're at ~$168/user. Method's CRM Enterprise is $73/user with native QB sync included."

**Portal:** "Need a customer portal for order status or online payments? Method includes it free. Zoho charges $125/month extra."

### Objection Handling

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "Zoho is cheaper" | "CRM alone is cheaper. For MWD, add CRM + Inventory + Books and you're at ~$168/user. Method is $27-$73/user with QB sync built in. The math changes fast." |
| "Zoho has inventory management" | "Zoho Inventory is a separate app at separate cost — and it doesn't connect to QBDT. Method surfaces your QB inventory data inside CRM workflows natively." |
| "We like one vendor for everything" | "The trade-off is managing multiple Zoho apps, each with its own interface. Method gives you one operational system with one expert who builds it for your business." |
| "Zoho has better marketing tools" | "True — Zoho's marketing suite is more mature. Method's strength is operational workflow and QB sync. Some customers use Method for operations and layer in a dedicated marketing tool." |

### Landmines

- Don't compare on CRM-only pricing — you'll lose. Always compare the full stack cost.
- Don't dismiss Zoho's ecosystem — it's genuinely broad. Redirect to depth, QB sync, and total cost.
- Don't forget the QBO angle — Zoho's QBO integration exists but is limited. Be specific about limitations, don't claim "no QBO integration."
