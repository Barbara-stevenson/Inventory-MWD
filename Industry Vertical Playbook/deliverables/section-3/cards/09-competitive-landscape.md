## Method's Position

**The only CRM with deep two-way QuickBooks sync that also handles operational workflows for MWD businesses at SMB pricing.** Every customer confirmed it — QB sync + customization are why they chose Method and why they stay.

## Partner Ecosystem: Specialized Tools Work *With* Method

Specialized inventory and manufacturing tools aren't competitors. Several Method customers run both:

- **Europena** — Fishbowl for deep inventory management + Method for CRM, audits, field workflows
- **SLE Technologies** — Method → SOS Inventory → QBO circular sync with API app routines and warehouse-level views
- **Cypress Engine** — Method for CRM pipeline + SOS Inventory for sales orders/inventory, with scheduled sync
- **Euroboor** — Full custom in-Method warehouse tracking: serial numbers, bin locations, multi-warehouse, back-order management
- **Custom warehouse tracking** — Customers have built warehouse inventory screens inside Method with manual "update warehouse" buttons and multi-location tracking

**The sales conversation:** "Need deep MRP with BOM, kitting, and lot tracking? Tools like Fishbowl or SOS Inventory handle that — and Method integrates with them. Need inventory visibility and warehouse tracking? We can build that inside Method."

## The Question That Determines Every Deal

**"Are you keeping QuickBooks?"**

| Their Answer | Your Position |
|---|---|
| "Yes, definitely" | **Strongest.** No competitor matches QB sync + ops workflows. Lead with it. |
| "We're open to switching" | **Weaker.** Enterprise ERP and Odoo become viable. Lead with cost, speed, customization. |
| "We've decided to move off QB" | **Weakest.** QB advantage gone. Compete on customization, pricing, DEP. |
| "We don't use QuickBooks" | **Not our ICP.** Qualify out unless Xero. |

## Competitive Positioning by Prospect Size

| Prospect Profile | Primary Competitors | Method's Best Angle |
|---|---|---|
| $2M-$5M, 10-20 employees | Zoho, HubSpot, spreadsheets | Affordable customization + QB sync |
| $5M-$15M, 20-50 employees | Odoo, Zoho, entry-level NetSuite | Enterprise capability without enterprise price |
| $15M-$30M, 50-100 employees | NetSuite, Salesforce, Dynamics | Cost + speed. Proof: Mobility City (54 locations), Europena ($25M) |
| $30M+, 100+ employees | NetSuite, Dynamics, SAP | Harder sell. Works if keeping QB + need operational CRM |

---

## Three Rules for Competitive Conversations

1. **Never trash-talk.** Acknowledge competitor strengths, then redirect to what matters for this buyer.
2. **Lead with the prospect's problem, not the competitor's weakness.** "Tell me about your quoting process" beats "Did you know NetSuite costs $25K to set up?"
3. **Know when to walk away.** If they need full MRP, are leaving QB, and have enterprise budget — be honest.
