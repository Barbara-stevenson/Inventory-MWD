## Why This Card Exists

MWD buyers are risk-averse. They've been burned by software that overpromised. Getting caught costs more than the deal — it costs trust, referrals, and reputation. If a prospect asks about something on this list, don't dodge it.

## The Honesty Framework

When a gap comes up, follow this pattern:

1. **Acknowledge** — "You're right, Method doesn't do [X] natively."
2. **Explain the alternative** — "What our customers do instead is [Y]."
3. **Provide proof** — "For example, [customer] handles it by [Z]."
4. **Redirect** — "The reason they chose Method despite that is [Method's unique value]."

**Never:** Dodge the question. Overpromise a roadmap feature. Claim "we can customize anything" without qualifying complexity. Make the prospect feel dumb for asking.

## Appendix: Detailed Gap Guidance

### Gap 1: Inventory Is QB Sync, Not Native

**What we don't do:** No native BOM, no kitting/assembly, no lot tracking, no shop floor control, no pick/pack/ship out of the box.

**How to handle:** Follow Card 05c exactly. Lead with honesty, offer both paths (third-party API or custom builds), match response to their actual need.

**Customer proof:** Europena (Fishbowl integration), SLE Technologies (SOS Inventory API sync), Euroboor (full custom in-Method warehouse tracking with serial numbers, bin locations), Duraline (custom equipment tracking), Vintage Makers (3,400+ SKUs via QBDT Enterprise).

### Gap 2: QBO vs. QBDT

**What QBO doesn't have:** No sales orders, no assembly items, no advanced inventory (multi-location, serial/lot, FIFO, barcode), no price levels, no units of measure, limited custom fields.

**Key line:** "The features Method can surface depend on your QuickBooks version. Desktop Enterprise gives you the most. QBO is simpler, so the data we work with is simpler too."

### Gap 3: No Native E-Commerce

**How to handle:** "Method doesn't have a native e-commerce integration today. If that's core, it would need custom integration or Zapier. For businesses where e-commerce is the primary channel, this may be a gap."

**When it matters:** Distributors selling through online channels, manufacturers with D2C.

### Gap 4: Email Marketing Throttles

**How to handle:** "Method handles transactional email and basic campaigns. For large-scale marketing, you'd want a dedicated email tool connected via Zapier."

**Customer proof:** Duraline experienced this with ~3,000 contacts.

### Gap 5: Reporting Requires Customization

**How to handle:** Frame as advantage: "Your dashboards show your KPIs — not someone else's template. DEP builds exactly what you need."

**Honest caveat:** Some customers still run certain reports in QuickBooks because Method's reporting is less intuitive for specific use cases.

### Gap 6: Performance at Scale

**How to handle:** Don't bring up proactively. If asked: "Like any highly customized system, there are performance considerations as complexity grows. DEP optimizes workflows to keep things running smoothly."

### Gap 7: EDI and Large-Scale Order Automation

**How to handle:** "EDI is specialized. Method handles standard quoting and invoicing, but automated EDI-compliant processing would need a dedicated tool alongside Method."

### Gap 8: Mobile Isn't Warehouse-Optimized

**How to handle:** "Method's mobile works great for field sales, customer visits, and on-the-road access. For warehouse floor operations — scanning, picking, packing — you'd want a dedicated WMS or custom build."

**Customer proof:** Vintage Makers uses mobile for field sales. Europena uses iPads for store-level audits. Neither is warehouse scanning.

### Gap 9: QB Sync Hiccups

**How to handle:** Don't bring up unless asked. "The sync is automatic and real-time in normal operation. In high-volume scenarios, you may occasionally need a manual re-sync. It's rare."

### Gap 10: Multi-Company Accounting

**How to handle:** "Method handles multi-company operations — Europena runs two mirrored entities. Accounting consolidation happens in QuickBooks."
