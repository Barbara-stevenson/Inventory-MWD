## When Method Wins / Loses

**Wins:** Prospect needs QuickBooks sync (Salesforce has none). Prospect is SMB (Manufacturing Cloud at $475/user prices them out). Needs operational workflows, not just sales pipeline. Doesn't have or want a Salesforce admin. Values simplicity over ecosystem.

**Loses:** Already in Salesforce ecosystem. Needs advanced demand planning (Manufacturing Cloud is enterprise-grade). Team has Salesforce experience. Needs massive integration ecosystem (4,000+ apps).

---

## The One Question

**"Which Salesforce product are you looking at?"** Sales Cloud is CRM — no production workflows. Manufacturing Cloud handles operations but costs $475/user/month. For a team of 8, that's $45,600/year before implementation.

## Appendix: Talk Track & Objection Handling

### Talk Track

**Opening:** "Salesforce is the biggest CRM out there — hard to argue with the brand. The question for a manufacturer or distributor is whether you need the Salesforce ecosystem, or operational CRM connected to QuickBooks. Those are very different things."

**MWD fit:** "Sales Cloud is CRM — great for pipelines, but no production workflows or inventory. Manufacturing Cloud does some of that, but at $475/user/month. For a team of 8, that's $45,600/year before implementation."

**QB question:** "Salesforce doesn't connect to QuickBooks. You'd need a third-party integration tool — typically $200-$500/month plus setup. And those integrations are rarely two-way in real time."

**Admin question:** "Who's going to manage Salesforce day-to-day? Complex orgs need a dedicated admin or consultant. Method's DEP gives you a dedicated expert at $325/month — no hiring required."

### Objection Handling

| They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "Everyone uses Salesforce" | "Enterprise companies do. For SMB manufacturers on QuickBooks, it's often overkill. Customers at $25M revenue with 54 locations chose Method over enterprise options because it fits how they actually work." |
| "Manufacturing Cloud is built for manufacturers" | "For enterprise manufacturers doing demand planning at scale. At $475/user, it's built for a different size business. Method handles the operational CRM and quote-to-cash that SMBs need, at a fraction of the cost." |
| "Salesforce has more integrations" | "AppExchange is massive. The trade-off is complexity — more integrations means more decisions and management. Method's focus is tighter: CRM, operations, QuickBooks. Everything connected without managing 15 apps." |
| "Our sales team knows Salesforce" | "Less training is a real advantage. The question is whether you need a sales CRM or operational CRM connected to QuickBooks." |

### Landmines

- Don't position Method as a Salesforce replacement — different markets, different tools.
- Don't dismiss the brand — prospects trust the name. Acknowledge, then redirect to fit and cost.
- Don't compare Manufacturing Cloud features one-to-one — its demand planning is enterprise-grade. Don't pretend otherwise.
