# MWD Marketing Toolkit — Team Walkthrough Script

**Format:** 15-minute team presentation
**Vibe:** Conversational, glanceable on your phone. Talking points, not a word-for-word script.
**Tip:** Each section has 2–3 key points. Hit those, and you're golden.

---

## 0:00–2:00 — Why We Built This

**Open with the problem:**

- We've been selling to MWD (Manufacturing, Warehousing, Distribution) without a shared playbook. Sales says one thing, marketing says another, nobody's sure what proof we actually have.
- This project fixes that. One toolkit, one source of truth, built on real data — not guesses.

**The headline:**

- We analyzed 465 prospect calls, enriched our Top 200 customer data, conducted 6 in-depth customer interviews, pulled from 1,097 account executive records, and integrated 7 published case studies from method.me.
- This isn't a branding exercise. It's a research-backed operating system for how we sell to MWD.

**Transition:** *"Let me walk you through what we actually built."*

---

## 2:00–5:00 — What's in the Marketing Toolkit (M1–M10)

**Pull up the toolkit if you can. If not, describe the layout:**

- 10 cards, each one a self-contained resource. Left sidebar to navigate. Click any card, get what you need.

**Hit the highlights — don't read all 10:**

- **M1: Core Messaging & Value Props** — Headlines, taglines, elevator pitches organized by our four pillars (Real-World Business, Quote-to-Cash, QuickBooks, Expert Support). Grab-and-go copy for any channel.
- **M2: Audience & Personas** — ICP profiles and buyer personas mapped to MWD. Who we're selling to, what they care about, how they talk about their problems.
- **M3: Customer Proof Library** — This is the big one for sales. Proof organized by use case: order management, quoting, fulfillment, inventory, reporting, integrations. Real quotes from real customers. Case study links. Grab-and-go proof statements you can drop into emails or decks.
- **M9: Prospect Voice Library** — What prospects are actually saying on calls. Pain frequencies, feature demands, objections — straight from 465 analyzed calls.
- **M10: Customization Showcase** — Screenshots and workflow examples showing the depth of what Method can do. Great for demos and proposals.

**The rest at a glance:**

- M4 covers competitive angles for content, M5 is a content playbook with calendar and distribution, M6 is a sales quick-reference cheat sheet, M7 is our Jobs-to-Be-Done framework, M8 is a Top 200 customer snapshot.

**Transition:** *"So that's the marketing toolkit. But we also built tools specifically for competitive selling."*

## 5:00–7:30 — Battlecards & Competitive Intel (S1–S4)

**Four battlecards, each for a different fight:**

- **S1: MWD Master Battlecard** — The overview. Covers all competitors at once. Start here if you're not sure who you're up against.
- **S2: vs. Enterprise (Salesforce / NetSuite)** — When the prospect is looking at the big guys. Our angle: they'll spend $25K+ on setup, wait 6+ months for implementation, lose their QuickBooks sync, and still need to hire admins. Method gives them enterprise-level customization at SMB pricing, live in weeks.
- **S3: vs. Mid-Market (Zoho / Odoo / Thryv)** — When they're comparing us to "good enough" platforms. Our angle: those tools either lack deep QB sync, can't truly customize workflows, or force you into their way of doing things.
- **S4: vs. Status Quo (Spreadsheets / QB Only)** — The most common competitor. Our angle: you're already spending those hours — you're just spending them on manual data entry, copy-paste between systems, and chasing down information.

**Key point:** Every battlecard has win themes, landmines to avoid, and honest gaps we own upfront. We don't pretend we're perfect — we get ahead of objections.

**Transition:** *"Now let me show you the strategic layer underneath all of this."*

**Transition:** *"So that's what exists. Here's how you actually use it day-to-day."*

## 9:30–12:00 — How to Use This (The Practical Part)

**For Marketing:**

- **Starting a campaign or writing content?** Start with M1 (messaging) and M5 (content playbook). All the approved copy and channel strategy is there.
- **Need proof for a landing page or email?** Go to M3 (Proof Library). Filter by use case, grab a quote, link a case study.
- **Writing competitive content?** M4 has the angles already framed. Don't start from scratch.

**For Sales:**

- **Before a call?** Check M6 (Sales Quick-Reference) for a one-page cheat sheet. Know the persona, the pains, the pitch.
- **Prospect mentions a competitor?** Pull the right battlecard (S1–S4). You've got win themes, traps to set, and honest answers for the hard questions.
- **Need proof mid-conversation?** M3 has grab-and-go proof statements organized by the exact pain your prospect just described.
- **Demoing customization?** M10 has real screenshots and workflow examples to show, not just tell.

**For Leadership:**

- **Strategic views** give you the market context: where we fit, where we win, where we're honest about gaps.
- **M8 (Top 200 Snapshot)** and **M9 (Prospect Voice Library)** are your data layer — what our best customers look like and what prospects are actually asking for.

**Transition:** *"Let me talk about the data behind all of this, because that's what makes it credible."*

## 12:00–13:30 — The Data Foundation

**This toolkit isn't opinion. It's built on:**

- **465 prospect call transcripts** — analyzed for pain points, feature demands, objections, and language patterns. This is what MWD buyers actually say when they're considering a CRM.
- **Top 200 customer data** — firmographic enrichment of Method's best MWD accounts. We know who our ideal customer actually looks like, not just who we think they are.
- **6 in-depth customer interviews** — Brown Safe (luxury manufacturing), Vintage Makers (wine cellars + distribution), Mobility City (54-location franchise), Duraline Systems (medical equipment), Europena (food distribution), GroGreen (field services). Real operators, real workflows.
- **1,097 account executive records** — sales activity data that informed our competitive positioning and win/loss analysis.
- **7 published case studies from method.me** — integrated directly into the Proof Library with links.
- **30+ web sources** — competitor research across Salesforce, NetSuite, HubSpot, Zoho, Odoo, Thryv, Jobber, Fishbowl, Katana, and Monday. Plus QuickBooks ecosystem research and TAM/SAM/SOM market sizing.

**The point:** When you use a proof statement from this toolkit, it's backed by data. When you make a competitive claim, it's been researched. This is the kind of credibility that closes deals.

## 13:30–15:00 — What's Next & Close

**Three things I want everyone to walk away with:**

1. **Bookmark the toolkit.** It's a single HTML file. Works in any browser, works on your phone. No login, no app, no friction.
2. **Start with M3 and the battlecards.** Those are the highest-impact pieces for anyone in a customer-facing role. If you only use two things, use those.
3. **The AI chatbot knows all of this.** If you need a quick answer — "what's our angle against Zoho for a warehouse distributor?" — ask it. It's trained on every document, interview, and research source in this project.

**Close:**

- This project took 7 sessions. We went from scattered docs and tribal knowledge to a single, research-backed operating system for MWD.
- It's not done forever — it'll evolve as we learn more, launch new products, and close more deals. But the foundation is solid, and it's ready to use today.
