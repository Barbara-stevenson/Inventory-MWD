# JTBD Analysis: Method App Catalog (Mode A)

## Work Orders

**Job story:** "When I need to assign and track field tasks for customers, I want to create and manage work orders with clear status and material tracking, so I can ensure jobs get completed on time and invoicing is accurate."

**Key desired outcomes:**
- Reduce time spent manually coordinating which jobs are assigned to whom
- Know the current status of any active job without asking around
- Minimize delays caused by unclear task assignments or scheduling conflicts
- Track materials used so I can bill accurately and avoid surprise costs
- Reduce the friction between job completion and customer billing

**Typical triggers:**
- A customer calls requesting service; someone needs to create a tracked task
- A field technician needs to know what jobs they're assigned for the day
- A manager wants visibility into how many jobs are in progress, pending, or completed
- Time to bill a customer for work done

---

## Estimates

**Job story:** "When I'm pitching work to a potential customer, I want to build and send a professional estimate quickly, so I can win deals faster and look professional."

**Key desired outcomes:**
- Reduce the time it takes to produce an estimate so I can respond to customer requests faster
- Minimize back-and-forth with the customer by sending clear, branded estimates
- Know which estimates are accepted, declined, or still pending
- Convert approved estimates into invoices or sales orders without re-entering data
- Keep estimate templates consistent and on-brand across the team

**Typical triggers:**
- A customer asks for a quote or proposal
- A sales rep needs to move a deal forward and formalize the scope
- Leadership needs to see which deals are in the "estimate" stage of the pipeline
- An estimate is approved and needs to become billable work

---

## Invoices

**Job story:** "When work is complete and I need to get paid, I want to create and send invoices that are synced with my accounting system, so I can track payment status and get paid faster."

**Key desired outcomes:**
- Reduce time spent creating invoices from scratch or manually copying data
- Decrease the gap between when work is done and when a customer receives an invoice
- Know exactly which invoices are paid, overdue, or partially paid
- Automate recurring billing for ongoing customer relationships
- Reduce manual bookkeeping by syncing invoices directly to QuickBooks
- Apply deposits and partial payments without breaking the accounting flow

**Typical triggers:**
- Work is complete (work order finished, estimate accepted)
- Weekly or monthly billing cycle arrives
- A customer makes a partial payment and you need to update records
- Accounting needs to close the books for the period

---

## Customers & Leads

**Job story:** "When I'm managing multiple customer relationships, I want to store all customer information, interactions, and history in one place, so I know who I'm talking to and don't lose track of anyone."

**Key desired outcomes:**
- Find customer information and communication history without searching multiple systems
- Reduce the risk of leads falling through the cracks due to poor tracking
- Segment and target customers for specific outreach without manual list-building
- See the full picture of a customer (past work, outstanding invoices, opportunities)
- Know which leads are most likely to convert based on interaction patterns
- Simplify the handoff when a lead becomes a customer

**Typical triggers:**
- New lead or customer comes in (phone call, website form, referral)
- Someone needs to recall past interactions with a specific customer
- Time to plan a sales campaign or targeted outreach
- A team member joins and needs customer context
- A customer calls with a complaint or new opportunity and you need their history

---

## Opportunities

**Job story:** "When I have several potential deals in progress, I want to track them through our sales pipeline with clear probabilities and close dates, so I can forecast revenue and prioritize which deals to focus on."

**Key desired outcomes:**
- See all active deals in one place and know which are closest to closing
- Reduce uncertainty about future revenue by estimating what deals are likely to close
- Prioritize follow-up activities based on deal probability and close date
- Understand which deals are stuck and need action
- Know which customers or contacts are responsible for which opportunities
- Report accurate pipeline health to leadership

**Typical triggers:**
- A new potential deal emerges and needs to be tracked
- Weekly team meeting where leaders review pipeline progress
- Month-end or quarter-end forecasting and reporting
- A customer shows interest in additional services or products
- An opportunity moves to a new stage (needs follow-up, decision pending, ready to close)

---

## Summary

These five apps represent core functions in a SMB's customer-facing business:

- **Customers & Leads** provides the foundation — the single source of truth for who you're doing business with
- **Opportunities** and **Estimates** support the pre-sales phase — qualifying leads, proposing solutions, and forecasting revenue
- **Work Orders** bridge execution — ensuring field teams have clear direction and status is visible to the whole company
- **Invoices** close the loop — converting delivered work into revenue and syncing it all back to accounting

The jobs they support are not about the apps themselves, but about the progress customers want to make: moving deals forward faster, staying organized as you scale, reducing manual work, and gaining visibility into what's happening in the business.
