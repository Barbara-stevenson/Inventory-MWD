# Method MWD Vertical Playbook — Structure Plan

**Last updated:** March 13, 2026

---

## What this is

A go-to-market playbook for Method CRM's Manufacturing, Warehousing & Distribution (MWD) vertical. The primary deliverable is the **MWD Marketing Toolkit** — a hub-and-spoke card system designed for anyone creating Method content for MWD.

The toolkit sits on top of deeper strategic research (Sections 1-3) that feeds it. Think of it as two layers: the toolkit is what marketers and sales use day-to-day, and the sections underneath are where the data and analysis live.

---

## Architecture

### Layer 1: MWD Marketing Toolkit (the daily-use resource)

Hub-and-spoke model — one central hub linking to 8 standalone cards. Each card works on its own. Pick it up, use it, put it back.

| Card | Title | What's in it |
|---|---|---|
| Hub | Toolkit Hub | Navigation, positioning summary, five rules, measurement KPIs, data freshness tracker |
| M1 | Core Messaging & Value Props | Headlines, taglines, copy-ready lines by pillar |
| M2 | Audience & Personas | Three personas, keyword themes, persona × content stage matrix |
| M3 | Customer Proof Library | Quotes, stats, stories organized by use case — tables with anonymized names (real names in brackets for internal) |
| M4 | Competitive Angles for Content | Positioning vs. NetSuite, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Odoo, Dynamics 365, Monday.com, Thryv, and the status quo |
| M5 | Content Playbook | Blog topics by intent, email sequences, social hooks, campaign ideas, trigger-based outreach angles |
| M6 | Sales Quick-Reference | Pricing, product overview, qualification triggers, inventory do/don't claims |
| M7 | Jobs to Be Done | Six core workflows MWD customers hire Method for, with flow diagrams and pillar mappings |
| M8 | Top 200 Snapshot | Demographics, revenue, verticals, customization depth, QB version across top 200 MWD accounts |

**Review format:** Single-page HTML with Apple whitepaper aesthetic, sidebar navigation, strategic diagram renderers. Generated by `gen_whitepaper.py`.

### Layer 2: Strategic Research (the foundation)

Three completed sections that fed the toolkit. These are the deep dives — referenced from the toolkit but not duplicated in it.

| Section | What's in it | Key cards |
|---|---|---|
| Section 1: Market & Customer Foundation | Market landscape, demand drivers, pain map, competitive positioning, pricing comparison, strategy brief, customer interviews, JTBD analysis, ICP & personas | 01a-01f, 02, 03, 04a-04d |
| Section 2: Positioning & Messaging | Positioning narrative, message house, inventory messaging, per-persona messaging, industry page copy, customer success stories | 05a-05c, 06, 07, 08 |
| Section 3: Competitive Intelligence | Competitive landscape, battlecards (NetSuite, Odoo, Zoho, Salesforce, HubSpot), honest gaps brief | 09, 10a-10e, 11 |

Each section has its own hub file and review HTML.

## Positioning anchors (non-negotiable)

- **Category:** "Customizable CRM" — not custom, not customized
- **Campaign line:** "Yes, There's a Method for That"
- **Four Yes Pillars:** Yes to real-world business, Yes to quote-to-cash, Yes to QuickBooks, Yes to expert support
- **Pricing:** $27 / $45 / $73 per user/month. DEP $325/month. Multi-Entity Admin $40/month.
- **Inventory:** Customization story, NOT native feature. Product has NOT launched. Several quarters away.
- **Tone:** Direct, conversational, grade 8 vocabulary. No buzzwords. Bold with cheekiness.
- **Customer names:** Anonymized externally, real names in [brackets] internally
